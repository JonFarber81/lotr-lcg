## Overview

This is a playtesting report for [[The Hunt for Gollum]], the first adventure pack in the [[Shadows of Mirkwood]] cycle. As the first episodic, or multi-part, quest introduced in the game, at the conclusion of this scenario we will not be catching Gollum — we will simply have the clues that we need to continue on our hunt for the wily little creature.

This quest has an interesting mix of challenges that will require careful planning and strategy to handle. Rather than be heavy on any one aspect (treacheries, locations, enemies) this scenario has a fairly even mix of everything, but it is the way that the encounter cards can synergize with each other that will most require us to be on our guard.

---

## Scenario Analysis

### Enemies

Most of the enemies in the encounter deck are weak, but in the case of the [[Hunters from Mordor]], they can become very strong, very quickly. With 2 defense and a whopping six hit points, we might be tempted to leave them alone, and worry about finishing them off later. This can be a devastatingly bad mistake to make. Because their threat and attack are each raised by 2 for each Clue card in play, they can very quickly become 6 threat, 6 attack monsters. A big part of our strategy for this quest will be predicated on handling these nasty fellows.

We may end up using [[Gandalf]] to weaken them in the staging area, then engage them and throw a chump blocker at them before finishing them off. Alternatively, in a nice bit of irony, we can trap the hunters in a [[Forest Snare]]. This is the preferred approach as it is not only strategically effective, but gives that extra bit of entertainment factor from seeing one of the Dark Lord's hunters helplessly caught with their leg in a trap. In such a situation, one can almost imagine Gollum somewhere in the shadows, just out of sight, sticking out his tongue and making faces as the hapless hunter becomes the prey.

Enemies like [[Misty Mountain Goblins]] and [[Goblintown Scavengers]] are laughably weak, but they have effects which either raise the threat in the staging area, or actually remove hard-fought progress from the current quest. We should not underestimate these vermin; their stats may not be impressive, but if we do not deal with them swiftly and with precision, they can succeed in their mission to waylay us from our hunt.

### Threat Management

We also need to be very careful with threat management. In particular, if we allow our threat to go over 35, [[Evil Storm]] can absolutely wreck our strategy of fielding an army of weak allies. Because both the first and second stages have us choosing and revealing additional cards from the encounter deck, we will need to be mindful of when it is safe to let this card trigger, and when it is best to just discard it. Between the extra cards and the synergistic effects, this scenario is very much designed to stall our questing efforts, putting us in much greater risk of an escalating threat and the negative effects that come along with that.

### Locations

This scenario also includes a plethora of locations that will try to clog up the staging area and slow us down. At 3 threat apiece, [[The West Bank]] and [[The East Bank]] are locations that we can't really afford to leave in the staging area, lest we risk location-lock and losing the game. However, both of these locations hamper our abilities during planning, if we choose to travel to them:

- **[[The West Bank]]** as active location will make key attachments like [[Steward of Gondor]] and [[Forest Snare]] that much harder to play.
- **[[The East Bank]]** makes allies cost an additional resource, a hindrance that an ally-heavy deck can ill afford to have if we are going to successfully field an army and quest to victory.

The golden rule with all of the locations in this quest is to travel to them when we can, as much as possible mitigate their effects, and only when we know for certain that we can explore them on our next turn.

[[The Old Ford]] is another nasty location that can cause us to get stuck, with threat equal to the number of allies in play. Hopefully we can avoid that location, but just in case we should try to save [[Secret Paths]] to cancel its threat on the round that it is revealed. We might be able to survive a round of getting stuck with some locations in the staging area ([[Gandalf]] and [[Faramir]] are great bail-outs for this), but if we get caught on either bank of the great river for too long, it is very unlikely that we will survive this quest.

### Treacheries

The encounter deck also features a treachery, [[Old Wives' Tales]], that will punish us for trying to save resources and hold heroes back for combat. With this in mind, the natural strategy for this quest will be to play as much as we can during planning, quest aggressively, and be careful about where we travel, all while looking out for the [[Hunters from Mordor]].

### Clue Objectives (Signs of Gollum)

We don't need a [[Signs of Gollum]] objective until we are ready to pass on to stage 3, and there are some nasty shadow effects that specifically target anyone with one of these objectives attached, so there is no reason to claim one of them any earlier. There are treacheries that can shuffle them back into the deck, but in general we shouldn't worry too much about getting a Clue when we need one. The ability to choose an encounter card from the top of the encounter deck at the beginning of the quest phase in the second stage, combined with Denethor's own scrying skills, should enable us to claim one of these objectives at the right time.

## Heroes and Key Strategy

**Heroes used:** [[Aragorn (Leadership)]], [[Theodred]], [[Denethor (Lore)]]

**Core strategy:** Power-questing — committing characters aggressively to progress quickly while managing threat carefully and using Denethor's scrying ability to control what comes off the encounter deck.

## Setup and Opening Hand

For this quest, the single most important thing is going to be cards that help us quest as quickly as possible. The opening hand drawn was:

- [[Valiant Sacrifice]]
- [[Forest Snare]]
- [[Gandalf]]
- [[Celebrian's Stone]]
- [[Self Preservation]]
- [[Faramir]]

This hand is perfect. Sure, there is no resource generation that will ultimately be needed to help pay for all of these cards, but the cards themselves are exactly what we need for power-questing. Between [[Celebrian's Stone]] and [[Faramir]], there should not be any problem questing very aggressively this game.

**Scenario setup:** Reveal 1 card from the encounter deck — [[River Ninglor]]. This location is yet another example of an obnoxious hindrance of the stated goal to quest aggressively. If left in the staging area, it will collect there with a bunch of other Riverland locations, only to haunt us later in the game. However, if we travel there, it will start removing progress from itself and the current quest stage. We will need to tread carefully and only travel to this location when we can mitigate its effects.

## Round-by-Round Play Log

### Round 1: Stage 1B (0 of 8 progress)
**Threat:** 28 (First Player)

**Hand:** [[Valiant Sacrifice]], [[Forest Snare]], [[Gandalf]], [[Celebrian's Stone]], [[Self Preservation]], [[Faramir]]

**Heroes:**
- [[Aragorn (Leadership)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
- [[Theodred]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (4 of 4 hit points)
- [[Denethor (Lore)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)

**Staging Area:** [[River Ninglor]]

**Draw:** [[Snowbourn Scout]] — a great card at this point: cheap, helps add a progress, and with [[Faramir]] at the lead, even the lowly scout can contribute to the quest. Later, he might even come in handy as a valiant sacrifice.

**Planning:** Pay 2 available Leadership resources to attach [[Celebrian's Stone]] to [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]. Since we know that later we will have need of [[Forest Snare]] and potentially [[Self Preservation]], we take the risk and have Denethor save his resource.

**Quest Phase:** Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] and [[Theodred]] to the quest. Trigger Theodred's ability to give Aragorn an extra resource, but we will not be triggering Aragorn's ability to allow him to be ready — we need to get [[Faramir]] into play as quickly as possible, so at this point we are saving resources and hoping to avoid [[Old Wives' Tales]].

**Staging:** Reveal [[Treacherous Fog]], which increases [[River Ninglor]]'s threat by 1, bringing total staging area threat to 3. With 5 willpower committed, we quest successfully and place 2 progress on stage 1. Because we quested successfully, we must look at the top 3 cards of the encounter deck and choose one to reveal: [[False Lead]] (which would surge), [[The Old Ford]] (bad news), and [[Pursued by Shadow]]. We reveal [[Pursued by Shadow]], which raises our threat by 1 since Denethor was not committed to the quest.

**Scrying:** Exhaust Denethor to trigger his ability. Top card is [[Banks of the Anduin]] — we leave it where it is. Knowing what is coming, we calculate we can clear [[River Ninglor]] if we travel there this round. At end of round it will remove 1 progress token from stage 1, but that's worth paying for removing 2 threat (and a Riverland) from the staging area.

**Travel:** [[River Ninglor]]

**End of Round:** Threat raises to 30. River Ninglor's effect removes 1 progress from stage 1 — **1 of 8 progress remaining.**

**Travel:** [[Eaves of Mirkwood]] (to free up the objective and clean up the staging area).

**Scrying:** Denethor peeks at [[The East Bank]]. As bad as this location can be, there are far worse cards in this encounter deck. Besides, locations free heroes (namely Denethor) to use their abilities instead of dealing with combat, so we leave it on top of the deck.

**End of Round:** Threat raises to 31.

### Round 3: Stage 1B (2 of 8 progress)

**Hand:** [[Valiant Sacrifice]], [[Forest Snare]], [[Gandalf]], [[Self Preservation]], [[Faramir]], [[Faramir]]

**Quest Phase:** Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]], [[Theodred]] and the [[Snowbourn Scout]]. After exploring [[Eaves of Mirkwood]], we place 2 progress on the quest stage — **4 of 8 progress.**

**Choosing encounter card:** Top 3 cards: [[Misty Mountain Goblins]], [[Old Wives' Tales]], and [[Goblintown Scavengers]]. Since resources are precious and the [[Misty Mountain Goblins]]' forced effect will only slow us down, we choose to reveal the [[Goblintown Scavengers]]. Since we are done with quest resolution, the threat they add to the staging area is meaningless, and we will be engaging them this round. However, we still have to discard the top card of our deck for their effect — sadly, the [[Steward of Gondor]] that we have been looking for has just been looted by the mangy scavengers and sits in the discard pile.

**Combat:** Engage the [[Goblintown Scavengers]]. Since we don't yet have any Clue objectives attached, it is safe to take an undefended attack. Their shadow card (Hunters from Mordor) would have done 3 damage to anyone with an objective attached, but in our case does nothing. Aragorn takes 1 damage from the undefended attack, then kills the scavengers on the counter-attack.

**Scrying:** Denethor peeks at the top card — another [[Signs of Gollum]]. Since each Clue is guarded, this card will bring another card as a guard from the encounter deck, in addition to making any hunters we see just that much nastier. We move this card to the bottom of the encounter deck.

**End of Round:** Threat raises to 32.

### Round 4: Stage 1B (4 of 8 progress)
**Threat:** 32 (First Player)

**Hand:** [[Valiant Sacrifice]], [[Forest Snare]], [[Gandalf]], [[Self Preservation]], [[Faramir]], [[Dunedain Mark]]

**Heroes:**
- [[Aragorn (Leadership)]]: 1 resource, 1 damage (4 of 5 hit points)
- [[Theodred]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (4 of 4 hit points)
- [[Denethor (Lore)]]: 4 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)

**Staging Area:** [[Banks of the Anduin]]
**Active Location:** [[The East Bank]]

**Draw:** [[Erebor Hammersmith]] — fortuitous, as some resource generation would really help speed up our questing efforts.

**Planning:** Because [[The East Bank]] is the active location, the [[Erebor Hammersmith]] will cost 3 Lore resources, but this is absolutely worth the extra cost. After playing the [[Erebor Hammersmith]], we return [[Steward of Gondor]] to our hand. Paying 1 resource from Aragorn and Theodred allows us to attach [[Steward of Gondor]] to Denethor — our resource generation woes are a thing of the past. We then trigger the Steward and spend 3 resources from Denethor to attach [[Self Preservation]] to Aragorn.

**Quest Phase:** Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]], [[Theodred]], the [[Erebor Hammersmith]], and the [[Snowbourn Scout]]. Again use the resource from Theodred to give Aragorn a resource, but leave him exhausted to save the resource for later. Trigger [[Faramir]] to improve all characters' questing ability.

**Staging:** Reveal [[Massing at Night]], which in a solo game essentially just does nothing but surge. For the additional encounter card: [[Gladden Fields]]. Yet another location.

With 10 total willpower against 4 threat, we make 6 progress. This allows us to explore [[The East Bank]] (allies are normal-price again!) and put 3 more progress on the stage — **7 of 8 progress, only 1 left to make to clear the first stage.**

**Choosing encounter card:** Top 3 cards: [[Eastern Crows]], [[Evil Storm]], and [[Signs of Gollum]]. With current threat of 32, we can safely reveal [[Evil Storm]] without any ill-effects.

**Travel:** [[Gladden Fields]] (will raise threat by 1 at end of round, but a certain wizard is about to help solve that problem).

**Scrying:** Denethor peeks — [[Misty Mountain Goblins]] on top. Ordinarily we want to avoid the forced effect from this enemy attacking. In this case, since we plan on clearing stage 1 next round and then won't have any progress on stage 2, this effect will be harmless. So we leave them on top of the encounter deck.

**Healing:** Aragorn still has 1 damage, so before refresh we exhaust [[Self Preservation]] to heal him back to full health.

**End of Round:** [[Gladden Fields]] pushes our threat to 34 — dangerously close to the critical 35 threshold.

### Round 5: Stage 1B (7 of 8 progress)
**Threat:** 34 (First Player)

**Hand:** [[Valiant Sacrifice]], [[Forest Snare]], [[Gandalf]], [[Faramir]], [[Dunedain Mark]]

**Heroes:**
- [[Aragorn (Leadership)]]: 2 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
- [[Theodred]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (4 of 4 hit points)
- [[Denethor (Lore)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)

**Staging Area:** [[Banks of the Anduin]]
**Active Location:** [[Gladden Fields]]

**Draw:** [[Guard of the Citadel]] — will come in handy shortly, as we want to start building up our ally army to push through to the final stage.

**Planning:** With help from [[Steward of Gondor]], pay 1 resource from Aragorn, 1 from Theodred, and 3 from Denethor to play [[Gandalf]]. After he enters play, lower our threat to **29** — more than offsetting the effect of [[Gladden Fields]], and keeping us safely out of range of some of the nastier cards in the encounter deck.

**Quest Phase:** We know only 2 threat is coming from the encounter deck, so there is no need to go overboard. Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] (who readies with a resource from Theodred) and [[Theodred]], then trigger Faramir's ability for a total of 7 willpower.

**Staging:** Reveal [[Misty Mountain Goblins]] — total 3 threat in staging area. This allows us to explore [[Gladden Fields]] and place the last progress on stage 1. **We move immediately to stage 2**, which fortunately does not require us to do anything until the beginning of the quest phase next round.

**Note:** Because we quested successfully, the forced effect on stage 1 still triggers, even though we ultimately moved on to the second stage. For the final time, we examine the top 3 cards of the encounter deck: two more copies of [[Gladden Fields]] and a [[Signs of Gollum]]. We don't want the staging area filling up with locations, particularly not ones that raise our threat if we travel to them. So we take the risk and reveal another [[Signs of Gollum]].

This decision might end up being a bad one — the guardian of this Clue objective is an [[Eastern Crows]], which immediately surges into a [[Hunters from Mordor]]. This is exactly the kind of situation we wanted to avoid, but at least we quested wisely and have characters held back to help with combat.

**Travel:** [[Banks of the Anduin]] — although it has 3 quest points and only 1 threat, an advantage of exploring this location next round is that it will give us one more card on top of the encounter deck that we know about.

**Combat:** Optionally engage the [[Hunters from Mordor]] (we have a certain trap waiting for them). The [[Misty Mountain Goblins]] and [[Eastern Crows]] also engage.

**Defenders and shadow cards:**
- [[Erebor Hammersmith]] defends against [[Eastern Crows]]. Shadow: [[Misty Mountain Goblins]]. Since we just moved to stage 2, there is no progress to remove — no effect.
- [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] defends against [[Misty Mountain Goblins]]. Shadow: another [[Hunters from Mordor]]. Since we have not claimed any Clue objectives yet, this shadow effect does nothing — glad to see another copy in the discard pile.
- [[Snowbourn Scout]] takes one for the team and blocks the [[Hunters from Mordor]]. Shadow: [[Pursued by Shadow]], which requires us to return an exhausted ally to our hand.

We don't want to return the scout, as the attack would then be undefended and kill one of our heroes. Fortunately the [[Erebor Hammersmith]] is already exhausted from defending, so we can return him to our hand (and we might even get to trigger his ability when we play him again). The 6 attack of the Hunters easily kills the poor [[Snowbourn Scout]].

**Paying for the sacrifice:** 1 resource from Aragorn to play [[Valiant Sacrifice]]. The two cards we draw: [[Secret Paths]] and [[Gleowine]]. Having additional card draw and a way to mitigate location threat will come in handy very soon.

**Counter-attacks:** [[Gandalf]] kills the [[Misty Mountain Goblins]]. Even if we had another character available to kill the [[Eastern Crows]], there would be no reason to do so — shuffling an enemy with surge back into the encounter deck makes absolutely no sense, particularly one so puny that we can safely take the damage undefended on Aragorn and then heal it with [[Self Preservation]].

**Scrying:** Denethor finds [[Goblintown Scavengers]] on top — fairly harmless, so we leave them.

**End of Round:** Threat increases to 30. Ready to take on stage 2.

### Round 6: Stage 2B (0 of 10 progress)
**Threat:** 30 (First Player)

**Hand:** [[Forest Snare]], [[Faramir]], [[Dunedain Mark]], [[Guard of the Citadel]], [[Erebor Hammersmith]], [[Secret Paths]], [[Gleowine]]

**Heroes:**
- [[Aragorn (Leadership)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
- [[Theodred]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (4 of 4 hit points)
- [[Denethor (Lore)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)

**Staging Area:** [[Signs of Gollum]]
**Active Location:** [[Banks of the Anduin]]

**Draw:** [[Protector of Lorien]] — combined with the extra cards from [[Gleowine]], this should allow us to push through these last two stages.

**Planning:** Trigger [[Steward of Gondor]] and pay 3 resources from Denethor to trap [[Hunters from Mordor]] in a [[Forest Snare]]. It's always a sweet irony when the hunter becomes the hunted. Aragorn and Theodred each pay 1 resource to pay for [[Guard of the Citadel]]. We need to get our ally army mustered as quickly as we can.

**Stage 2 special rule:** We must choose and reveal one of the top two cards from the encounter deck at the beginning of the quest phase. This is one less card than in stage 1, but we have the advantage that it happens before we commit characters to the quest. The cards are: [[Goblintown Scavengers]] (scried last round) and [[West Bank]]. Since we have the staging area free of locations, it should be safe to add one, and we have no way of knowing how much threat the scavengers will add or which of our cards they will loot — so we add [[West Bank]] to the staging area.

**Quest Phase:** Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]], [[Theodred]] and the [[Guard of the Citadel]]. Aragorn will stay exhausted and we trigger Theodred's ability to give him a resource. Trigger [[Faramir]] to give each character an additional willpower.

**Staging:** Reveal [[Old Wives' Tales]], which discards the resource from Aragorn and exhausts Denethor. With 9 willpower against 3 threat, we make 6 progress. [[Banks of the Anduin]] is explored (goes back on top of the encounter deck) and we make 3 progress on stage 2 — **3 of 10 progress.**

We don't want to risk traveling to [[West Bank]] right now — next round we should be able to use [[Secret Paths]] to cancel the threat from this location, so we safely ignore it for now.

**Combat:** All our characters are exhausted, so the [[Eastern Crows]]' attack will be undefended. Fortunately the shadow card (another [[Hunters from Mordor]]) has no effect, so Aragorn takes 1 point of damage. We immediately exhaust [[Self Preservation]] to heal the damage. The [[Hunters from Mordor]] engaged with us cannot attack (trapped in [[Forest Snare]]), so his shadow card ([[Banks of the Anduin]]) is discarded without effect.

**End of Round:** Threat raises to 31.

### Round 7: Stage 2B (3 of 10 progress)
**Threat:** 31 (First Player)

**Hand:** [[Faramir]], [[Dunedain Mark]], [[Erebor Hammersmith]], [[Secret Paths]], [[Gleowine]], [[Protector of Lorien]]

**Heroes:**
- [[Aragorn (Leadership)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
- [[Theodred]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (4 of 4 hit points)
- [[Denethor (Lore)]]: 1 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)

**Staging Area:** [[Signs of Gollum]], [[West Bank]]
**Active Location:** None

**Draw:** [[Henamarth Riversong]] — cheap allies are a welcome sight right now.

**Planning:** Trigger [[Steward of Gondor]], then Denethor pays 2 for [[Gleowine]] and 1 to attach [[Protector of Lorien]] to Aragorn.

**Stage 2 card choice:** Before committing characters, look at the top two cards: [[River Ninglor]] or [[Treacherous Fog]]. We don't need any more locations in the staging area, and we plan on cancelling the threat from [[West Bank]] anyway, so we go ahead and reveal [[Treacherous Fog]].

**Quest Phase:** Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] to the quest (he spends a resource to ready), along with [[Theodred]], [[Denethor (Lore)|Denethor]], and [[Guard of the Citadel]]. Theodred gives a resource to Denethor. Just in case another [[Old Wives' Tales]] is about to be revealed, we immediately pay 1 from Denethor to play [[Secret Paths]] on [[West Bank]] — that location now contributes no threat for the remainder of the phase. Trigger [[Faramir]] to give each character +1 willpower.

**Staging:** Reveal another [[West Bank]].

**Rules nuance:** According to the latest FAQ, [[Treacherous Fog]] actually creates a continuous effect (until the end of the phase) in the staging area that adds +1 threat to each location, even ones revealed after [[Treacherous Fog]]. This means the [[West Bank]] just revealed will contribute 4 threat to the staging area. Fortunately the original [[West Bank]] is not contributing any threat, and with our 11 willpower we make enough progress to complete this stage of the quest.

Because we quested successfully, we can trigger the response on [[Signs of Gollum]] and attach it to Theodred. We then move on to the final quest stage.

**Travel:** [[West Bank]]. It makes attachments and events more expensive, but we don't have any in hand that we actually need to play, and [[The Old Ford]]'s shadow effect was a chilling reminder of the risk we run if we let the staging area fill up with Riverlands locations.

**Combat:** Aragorn defends against [[Eastern Crows]]. Their shadow card is [[The Old Ford]], which — if there had been one more Riverland location in play — would have wiped out two of our allies! Fortunately, everyone is safe, and Aragorn does not take any damage.

**After combat:** Trigger [[Gleowine]]'s ability to draw a [[Secret Paths]]. Traveling to [[West Bank]] doesn't look like as good of an idea now — but resource generation saves the day. With [[Steward of Gondor]] attached to Denethor, we will still be able to afford [[Secret Paths]] even at its new, higher cost.

**End of Round:** Threat raises to 32. Ready to win the game.

## Closing Thoughts

In this bear's humble opinion, [[The Hunt for Gollum]] is certainly not the best-designed quest in the [[Shadows of Mirkwood]] cycle, but it does serve as a good test of our strategic skills. Having to manage our threat, handle locations with nasty travel and active effects, and deal with enemies that synergize with other encounter cards are all skills that we will be putting to use as we continue on our adventures along Beorn's Path.
