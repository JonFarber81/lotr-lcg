## Overview

With our [[Journey Along the Anduin]] complete, it is now time to look ahead to making an [[Escape from Dol Guldur]]. This is seriously going to test our mettle — even with all of the cards printed after the Core Set, [[Escape from Dol Guldur]] remains one of the most difficult scenarios in the game.

Because one hero is held prisoner and unavailable for the first stage, this scenario is exceedingly difficult to defeat solo. With three guarded objectives in the staging area at setup, the difficulty can be overwhelming. For this reason, the author recommends a **two-deck approach** rather than trying to tackle the scenario solo.

In Part 4, the Leadership/Lore deck was built around [[Aragorn (Leadership)]], [[Theodred]], and [[Glorfindel (Lore)]]. This article covers the companion **Tactics/Spirit deck** designed to complement it.

---

## The Challenge: Escape from Dol Guldur

[[Escape from Dol Guldur]] scales very poorly for solo play. The core problem: one hero begins the scenario imprisoned, leaving you with only two heroes for the first stage. Given that the Core Set does not offer much in the way of cards to mitigate this, a two-player approach where each player brings a full deck is far more reliable.

Key setup challenges:
- One hero imprisoned and unavailable at start
- Three guarded objectives in the staging area at setup
- The [[Nazgul of Dol Guldur]] is a powerful enemy requiring significant combat output to defeat
- Encounter deck is unforgiving and punishing

---

## Hero Selection

In picking three heroes, the focus is on two orthogonal but equally important goals:
1. **Emphasize the strengths of the Tactics and Spirit spheres**
2. **Offset the weaknesses of the companion Leadership/Lore deck**

The Leadership/Lore deck is strong at questing and resource generation, but its combat output is limited. The Tactics/Spirit deck must provide raw combat power to compensate.

### [[Gimli]]
**Sphere:** Tactics | **Threat:** 11

Gimli is chosen as the primary attacker. The son of Gloin has one of the best passive effects in the game: the more damage he takes, the more powerful his attack becomes. Each point of damage he has taken raises his attack by 1, so a fully loaded Gimli with [[Citadel Plate]] can accumulate up to 8 damage tokens, bringing his total attack to 10.

With [[Citadel Plate]] equipped and [[Unexpected Courage]] attached, Gimli can block and kill most enemies in the game without any help. Against the [[Nazgul of Dol Guldur]], this setup allows Gimli to defeat the enemy after only two rounds, and without assistance from the other deck.

The key synergy: Gimli *wants* to take damage. The more hurt he is, the more dangerous he becomes — a mechanic that rewards aggressive play and makes him uniquely suited to the punishment [[Escape from Dol Guldur]] dishes out.

### [[Thalin]]
**Sphere:** Tactics | **Threat:** 9

Thalin is chosen as the second Tactics hero over [[Legolas]] for a specific reason: his ability triggers on encounter deck reveals, not on kills. When an enemy is revealed from the encounter deck, Thalin deals 1 damage to it automatically. This:

- Can outright kill enemies with only 1 hit point (like [[Eastern Crows]])
- Weakens tougher enemies so allies like [[Gondorian Spearman]] can finish them off more easily
- Synergizes with [[Swift Strike]] to kill enemies before they even engage

"Being able to instantly dispatch small, obnoxious enemies like Eastern Crows gives the dwarf one of the most unique abilities" in the Core Set.

Thalin's weakness: he doesn't have a combat-focused action ability himself, so he very often does nothing but commit to the quest each round. He is essentially a 2-willpower quester who happens to also weaken or kill enemies as they appear. In this deck, that passive value is exactly what is needed.

### [[Eowyn]]
**Sphere:** Spirit | **Threat:** 9

Eowyn, the shield-maiden of Rohan, has the highest printed willpower of any hero in the game at 4 willpower. She serves as the deck's primary quester, contributing massively to the quest phase each round.

Additionally, her action ability allows you to discard a card from your hand to give her +1 willpower for the round, or to use her attack. This makes her a flexible emergency attacker when needed, and also provides a safety valve for clearing unneeded cards from your hand.

**Starting Threat:** Gimli (11) + Thalin (9) + Eowyn (9) = **29 total threat**

This is a high starting threat, which is one reason [[The Galadhrim's Greeting]] is important in the deck.

## Deck Composition

### Design Philosophy

This deck intentionally deviates from the traditional guideline of including roughly 20 allies (50% of the player deck). The Tactics and Spirit spheres offer such powerful attachments and events that forcing 20 allies would mean including high-cost or low-powered cards unnecessarily.

> "This is a great example of where it is important to be flexible when designing a deck. If we insisted on including 20 allies in this deck, not only would we be forced to include high-cost or low-powered allies, but we would be artificially limiting the number of quality attachments and events that we are playing."

When your sphere's best cards are attachments and events, lean into that strength rather than padding the ally count to hit an arbitrary threshold. Rules of thumb are starting points, not constraints.

## Card-by-Card Analysis

### Allies

#### [[Gondorian Spearman]] (×3)
Cost 2, Tactics. A bargain at only 2 resources. Synergizes naturally with Thalin: if Thalin has already dealt 1 damage to an incoming enemy, the Spearman's "when this ally defends, deal 1 damage to the attacker" ability can finish off enemies with 2 hit points before they even resolve their attack. Excellent cheap blocker and support attacker.

#### [[Veteran Axehand]] (×3)
Cost 2, Tactics. Cheap, efficient Tactics ally with 2 attack. Provides early-game combat support and helps chip damage onto enemies before Gimli needs to engage. The backbone of the early combat plan.

#### [[Horseback Archer]] (×2)
Cost 3, Tactics. Ranged attacker. The Ranged keyword allows this ally to attack enemies engaged with the other player in multiplayer, extending the deck's offensive reach across the table.

#### [[Northern Tracker]] (×2)
Cost 4, Spirit. The ability to clear multiple locations from the staging area is too good to pass up. Location control is a critical concern in many scenarios, and the Northern Tracker is the Core Set's best answer to location lock. Two copies ensure you see him reliably.

#### [[Lorien Guide]] (×1)
Cost 3, Spirit. Single copy for additional location control. Slower than Northern Tracker but contributes to keeping the staging area manageable. 

#### [[Gandalf]] (×1)
Cost 5, Neutral. An expensive, game-altering play. Use Gandalf strategically: reduce threat when it gets too high, draw cards when you need options, or deal 4 damage to a target enemy to soften it up. Save resources for the right moment. Only one copy because you can't always afford him, but his flexibility is unmatched.

#### [[Beorn]] (×1)
Cost 6, Neutral. Another expensive, game-altering play. Beorn's massive attack and hit points make him a devastating combat ally when you can afford him. Like Gandalf, he is a "save up and use at the right moment" card — not an early-game play. One copy is sufficient.

### Attachments

#### [[Citadel Plate]] (×2)
Cost 4, Tactics. The cornerstone of the Gimli engine. Grants Gimli +4 hit points, raising his total to 9. Those extra hit points become extra attack power via his passive ability. A fully damaged Gimli with Citadel Plate has 8 damage tokens, giving him 8+2 = 10 total attack. Can also be played on [[Aragorn (Leadership)]] in the companion deck in multiplayer to boost his defense or survivability.

> "In multi-player games, you are not limited to only playing attachments on your own characters." Tactics resources can pay for Citadel Plate on characters in the other deck, enabling cross-deck synergies.

#### [[Dwarven Axe]] (×2)
Cost 2, Tactics. Simple +1 attack weapon for Gimli or any other Dwarf character. Cheap and efficient. Stacks with Gimli's passive to push his attack higher even earlier in the game.

#### [[Blade of Gondolin]] (×2)
Cost 1, Tactics. Extremely cheap weapon that also generates progress tokens on locations when the bearer kills an enemy. Additional location management on top of Northern Tracker.

#### [[Horn of Gondor]] (×1)
Cost 1, Tactics. Generates a resource whenever an ally leaves play (including when they are destroyed or returned to hand). In a deck with several allies, this provides meaningful resource recovery. One copy is enough given its situational value.

#### [[Favor of the Lady]] (×2)
Cost 2, Spirit. Grants +1 willpower to a hero. Target Eowyn to push her questing power even higher, or distribute to other heroes to boost overall quest contribution. A straightforward willpower accelerant.

#### [[Unexpected Courage]] (×1)
Cost 2, Spirit. Grants an additional action to a hero. The primary target is Gimli: with Unexpected Courage, Gimli can both defend (taking damage to power up his attack) and then attack the same enemy in the same round. This is the combo that allows Gimli to defeat the [[Nazgul of Dol Guldur]] solo in two rounds. Only one copy in the Core Set, and it belongs here.

---

### Events

#### [[Feint]] (×2)
Cost 1, Tactics. Prevents an enemy from attacking this round. Invaluable when facing high-attack enemies that would otherwise overwhelm your defenders. Buys time to build up Gimli's damage or save a critical defender. Use it to survive the rounds before Citadel Plate comes online.

#### [[Quick Strike]] (×2)
Cost 1, Tactics. Allows a character to immediately attack an engaged enemy, resolving before the enemy's attack. "Quick Strike is amazing with Gimli, as it allows him to kill an engaged enemy before the foe has a chance to strike." Can also be used in a cross-deck context when the other player has engaged an enemy that Gimli can help finish off.

#### [[Swift Strike]] (×1)
Cost 0, Tactics. Free event that attacks an enemy in the staging area. Pairs beautifully with Thalin: if Thalin's reveal-damage has reduced an enemy to 1 hit point, Swift Strike kills it before it even engages, preventing its attack entirely. Cost 0 makes it essentially risk-free.

#### [[A Test of Will]] (×2)
Cost 1, Spirit. "Nothing has since replaced the effectiveness of A Test of Will." Cancels a treachery card when revealed from the encounter deck. Essential in almost every Spirit deck. [[Escape from Dol Guldur]] has particularly brutal treacheries, making this card invaluable. Two copies to ensure access.

#### [[Hasty Stroke]] (×2)
Cost 0, Spirit. Free treachery cancellation for shadow cards. Prevents a shadow effect from triggering when an enemy attacks. Protects against devastating shadow effects that could discard key attachments, deal unexpected damage, or return enemies to the staging area.

#### [[The Galadhrim's Greeting]] (×2)
Cost 4, Spirit. Reduces each player's threat by 6 (or just your own for 1 less cost). Essential for managing the deck's high starting threat of 29. The Leadership/Lore deck has Sneak Attack + Gandalf for threat reduction; this deck uses Galadhrim's Greeting as its primary threat control. Two copies provide reliability.

#### [[Dwarven Tomb]] (×1)
Cost 1, Spirit. Returns a Spirit card from your discard pile to your hand. The primary target is a used [[The Galadhrim's Greeting]], enabling a second large threat reduction from a single copy. Also works to recycle [[A Test of Will]] or [[Hasty Stroke]] in emergencies.

#### [[A Light in the Dark]] (×2)
Cost 1, Spirit. Returns an enemy engaged with you to the staging area, ending its attack. A powerful defensive event that gives you breathing room when overwhelmed. Particularly useful when you've drawn too many enemies or when a high-attack foe has engaged before you're ready.

#### [[Stand and Fight]] (×3)
Cost 2, Spirit. Puts an ally from any player's discard pile into play. Three copies because this event creates powerful synergy across both decks: if the Leadership/Lore deck discards a strong ally, Stand and Fight can pull it into play on the Tactics/Spirit side, or vice versa. Also interacts with Sneak Attack in the other deck — an ally Sneak Attacked into play could be retrieved by Stand and Fight after it leaves.

## Two-Deck Synergy

This Tactics/Spirit deck is specifically designed as the complement to the Leadership/Lore deck from Part 4. The synergies between them include:

| Synergy | How It Works |
|---|---|
| [[Citadel Plate]] on [[Aragorn (Leadership)\|Aragorn]] | Tactics resources pay for armor on the Leadership hero |
| [[Horseback Archer]] assists Leadership side | Ranged allows attacking enemies engaged with the other player |
| [[Quick Strike]] across decks | Gimli can kill enemies engaged with the other player |
| [[Stand and Fight]] across decks | Pulls discarded allies from either discard pile |
| [[Gandalf]] threat management | Coordinates with the Leadership deck's own Gandalf plays |
| [[Unexpected Courage]] on Gimli | Enables Gimli to both defend and counterattack, taking pressure off the other deck's defenders |

The Leadership/Lore deck handles resource generation, card draw, and questing stability. The Tactics/Spirit deck handles combat threats, enemy elimination, and threat control. Together they cover each other's blind spots.

## Key Strategies and Tips

### The Gimli Engine
Get [[Citadel Plate]] and [[Unexpected Courage]] onto Gimli as fast as possible. Once online, this combination allows Gimli to:
1. Defend an attack (taking damage, powering up his attack)
2. Use Unexpected Courage to ready
3. Attack the enemy back with his now-enhanced attack value

Against the [[Nazgul of Dol Guldur]], this engine defeats him without help in approximately two combat rounds.

### Thalin + Swift Strike Combo
When an enemy is revealed from the encounter deck:
1. Thalin deals 1 automatic damage
2. If the enemy has 2 or fewer hit points, play [[Swift Strike]] (free) to attack it before it engages
3. Enemy is eliminated before it ever attacks you

This combo neutralizes small enemies completely and is devastating against [[Eastern Crows]] and other nuisance enemies.

### Threat Management
Starting threat of 29 means aggressive threat escalation from round 1. Prioritize playing [[The Galadhrim's Greeting]] before reaching threat 40-45. Use [[Dwarven Tomb]] to recycle it when needed. Coordinate with the Leadership/Lore deck's Gandalf plays to stagger threat reduction across multiple rounds.

### Location Control
[[Northern Tracker]] and [[Blade of Gondolin]] handle location management — a critical concern since Tactics has no native location-clearing ability. Get Northern Tracker into play early and keep locations from accumulating in the staging area.

### Stand and Fight Recursion
With three copies of [[Stand and Fight]], you can repeatedly recycle powerful allies from either deck's discard pile. In extended games, this turns discarded allies into a renewable resource.

### Flexible Ally Deployment
[[Beorn]] and [[Gandalf]] are "save and spike" plays. When the game situation calls for a dramatic turnaround — a burst of damage, a sudden threat drop, or a surge of card draw — these allies deliver. Don't play them early just because you can afford them; save them for when their impact is maximized.

## Deviation from the 50% Allies Rule

A key lesson from this deck: **the 50% allies guideline is just a starting point.** When a sphere's best tools are attachments and events rather than allies, force yourself to include quality over quantity.

The Tactics sphere has exceptional events (Feint, Quick Strike, Swift Strike) and powerful attachments (Citadel Plate, Dwarven Axe, Blade of Gondolin). The Spirit sphere has outstanding events (A Test of Will, Hasty Stroke, The Galadhrim's Greeting). Padding the ally count to hit 20 would mean including weaker cards and crowding out these powerhouses.

Build toward the deck's actual win condition, not toward arbitrary structural ratios.

## Summary

This Tactics/Spirit deck built around [[Gimli]], [[Thalin]], and [[Eowyn]] provides:
- **Combat power:** Gimli's damage-scaling passive, boosted by Citadel Plate, makes him a one-dwarf killing machine
- **Enemy suppression:** Thalin weakens or kills enemies as they're revealed, enabling efficient cleanup
- **Questing:** Eowyn's 4 willpower anchors the quest phase
- **Threat control:** Galadhrim's Greeting and Dwarven Tomb manage the elevated starting threat
- **Location control:** Northern Tracker and Blade of Gondolin keep the staging area clear
- **Treachery cancellation:** A Test of Will and Hasty Stroke protect against encounter deck punishment
- **Cross-deck synergy:** Horseback Archer, Quick Strike, Stand and Fight, and Citadel Plate all create value across both decks

Together with the Leadership/Lore deck from Part 4, this combination is well-positioned to tackle the challenges of [[Escape from Dol Guldur]].
