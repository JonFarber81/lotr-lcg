## Introduction

At our last stop on Beorn's Path, we successfully cleared the Carrock of trolls. Continuing on the path, we are now making [[A Journey to Rhosgobel]], the home of Radagast the Brown. While traveling the Anduin valley, we discover Wilyador, an eagle that is gravely injured. Our goal is now to bring Wilyador to Rhosgobel as quickly as possible, with the hope that the wizard can save him.

---

## Scenario Overview

This scenario is quite different in terms of the specific challenges that it presents. Wilyador starts the game with 2 wounds and at the end of each round he receives two more wounds. While the eagle possesses an impressive 20 hit points, we need to be able to heal him completely by the time we pass the second quest stage. Otherwise, Wilyador will be too far gone for even Radagast's skills in healing and we will lose the quest.

Many treacheries in the encounter deck will specifically single out the eagle and the 2 damage he receives at the end of each round is effectively a game clock. Unless we find other means of healing our eagle friend, he will die in **9 rounds**.

### Key Constraints

- Attachments **cannot be played on Wilyador**, so [[Self Preservation]] will not work to mitigate the damage he takes each round.
- Of cards in the core set and the three available Adventure Packs, **only [[Lore of Imladris]] can target Wilyador**, so we will definitely be including that in our revised deck.

---

## Key Challenges

### Healing

Questing quickly will be one of our priorities so that we can keep damage to the eagle to a minimum. Without access to the Spirit sphere, we cannot cancel any treacheries that come from the encounter deck, so we will quest aggressively and hope for a bit of luck.

### Athelas Objectives

Athelas leaves can be found as an objective in this scenario. Aside from [[Lore of Imladris]], they represent our only option for healing the eagle. These cards can easily end up on the bottom of the encounter deck, or be **discarded as shadow cards from attacking enemies**, which is where a lot of the luck of this scenario comes into play. In any case, we will be taking full advantage of Denethor's ability to try and maintain some semblance of control over this random factor.

With Wilyador limited to being healed 5 damage from each Athelas or [[Lore of Imladris]], we will need to find multiple copies of these two cards to have any chance of saving the eagle's life. While card draw can help us find our [[Lore of Imladris]], we are left to the hands of fate for how many of the healing leaves we can collect. Ultimately we have no choice but to quest aggressively; the fewer the rounds of automatic damage and exposure to nasty treacheries, the more likely we are to be able to heal Wilyador completely.

### Flying Enemies and Ranged

One last concern that we must keep in mind is the several copies of **flying enemies that can only be defended or attacked with ranged characters**. Coupling with these enemies, many shadow effects in the encounter deck require that damage from undefended attacks be applied to Wilyador instead of a hero. For this reason we will want to avoid undefended attacks at all cost. Accordingly, our deck will include more ranged characters, and attachments that grant the ranged keyword.

---

## Deck Modifications

As far as changes to the deck are concerned, we will focus on healing and the ranged keyword. Since the mix of cards is shifting more towards the Lore sphere with these changes, we will also be adding a song to help with resource-smoothing. In addition to having ranged, [[Haldir of Lórien]] has excellent willpower, making him the perfect ally to recruit for this quest.

To make room for these changes, we will be removing some of the more expensive leadership allies as well as stat-boosting events and attachments. Assuming we can get one or two characters with ranged into play, the enemies in this quest are not particularly formidable. The treacheries, on the other hand, are much more likely to pose problems. The allies that we remove have low willpower, so their absence should not negatively impact our ability to quest.

## Setup and Opening Hands

Because of the special nature of this scenario, our priorities will be a bit different. While resource generation and card draw are as important as always, **healing is of the utmost importance** for saving Wilyador. Specifically, [[Lore of Imladris]] will allow us to heal the eagle, even if we cannot find enough Athelas.

**First opening hand (mulligan'd):** [[Haldir of Lórien]], [[Protector of Lórien]], [[Faramir]], [[Song of Wisdom]], [[Erebor Hammersmith]], [[Dúnedain Warning]]

Lacking card draw, resource generation or healing, we take a mulligan and hope for a better hand.

**Final opening hand (kept):** [[Song of Kings]], [[Steward of Gondor]], [[Forest Snare]], [[Haldir of Lórien]], [[Snowbourn Scout]] x2

## Game Report

### Round 1: Stage 1B (0 of 8 progress)

**28 threat (First Player)**

```
Hand: Song of Kings, Steward of Gondor, Forest Snare, Haldir, Snowbourn x2
— Aragorn: 1 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
— Theodred: 1 resource, 0 damage (4 of 4 hit points)
— Denethor: 1 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)
Staging Area: Rhosgobel
Wilyador: 2 damage (18 of 20 hit points)
```

After collecting resources we draw [[Protector of Lórien]]. This is actually a really good card for this quest, especially if we can find some card draw to help fuel the ability. Questing is going to be of the utmost importance to complete this scenario quickly.

**Planning:** Play [[Steward of Gondor]] on Denethor and exhaust it to give him an impressive first-turn total of 3 resources. Pay 1 of those resources to attach [[Protector of Lórien]] to Aragorn. Because of his ability to ready after committing to a quest, we may even be able to get double duty from this card, boosting both his willpower and defense.

**Quest Phase:** Commit Aragorn, Theodred and Wilyador. Use Theodred's ability to add 1 resource to Aragorn. While it is dangerous to exhaust Wilyador to quest, we take the risk to help complete the first stage as quickly as possible.

**Staging:** Reveal an Athelas guarded by Swarming Insects. Wilyador and Theodred each take 1 damage for not having attachments, but we make 3 progress on stage 1 and now have one of the precious Athelas, unguarded in the staging area.

**Denethor scry:** Top card is Dol Guldur Beastmaster — an enemy we should be able to handle. We keep it on top and plan accordingly.

*End of round: +2 damage to Wilyador, +1 threat.*

---

### Round 2: Stage 1B (3 of 8 progress)

**29 threat (First Player)**

```
Hand: Song of Kings, Forest Snare, Haldir, Snowbourn x2
— Aragorn: 2 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
— Theodred: 1 resource, 1 damage (3 of 4 hit points)
— Denethor: 3 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)
Staging Area: Rhosgobel, Athelas (unguarded)
Wilyador: 5 damage (15 of 20 hit points)
```

Draw: [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]]. While this card will help keep our heroes at full strength, we really want to find a copy of [[Lore of Imladris]] to help our ailing eagle friend.

**Planning:** Attach [[Song of Kings]] to Denethor, then play [[Haldir of Lórien]] and a [[Snowbourn Scout]]. Trigger the scout's response and add 1 progress to Rhosgobel.

**Quest Phase:** Quest with Aragorn, Theodred, Wilyador and [[Haldir of Lórien]]. Ready Aragorn with the bonus resource from Theodred.

**Staging:** Reveal the Dol Guldur Beastmaster — prepare for a fight. Before resolving the quest, discard [[Forest Snare]] and [[Snowbourn Scout]] to [[Protector of Lórien]], giving Aragorn +2 willpower until end of phase. Result: **5 progress on stage 1**, pushing us on to **stage 2**. Now we can finally heal our eagle ally.

**Combat:** Optionally engage the Dol Guldur Beastmaster. The [[Snowbourn Scout]] defends against the Beastmaster (dealt two shadow cards). Shadow cards: Great Forest Web and Mirkwood Flock — no effect since attack was not undefended, but the Beastmaster's 3 attack kills the scout. Aragorn counter-attacks the Beastmaster for 2 damage.

**Denethor scry:** Forest Grove on top. This location is precisely what we need to find more Athelas, so we keep it there.

*End of round: +2 damage to Wilyador (now 7 damage). Raise threat.*

### Round 3: Stage 2B (0 of 12 progress)

**30 threat (First Player)**

```
Hand: Daughter of the Nimrodel
— Aragorn: 2 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
— Theodred: 1 resource, 1 damage (3 of 4 hit points)
— Denethor: 2 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)
Active Location: Rhosgobel
Engaged: Dol Guldur Beastmaster
Staging Area: Athelas (unguarded)
Wilyador: 7 damage (13 of 20 hit points)
```

Draw: [[Faramir]] — a welcome sight; his ability will make our strategy of aggressive questing that much easier to execute.

**Planning:** Play both [[Faramir]] and [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]]. We need to try to clear this stage in the next few rounds.

**Quest Phase:** Keep some character back for combat. Commit Aragorn, Theodred, Wilyador and the [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]]. Ready Aragorn with the resource from Theodred. After adding the expected Forest Grove to the staging area, we explore Rhosgobel and add it to our victory display. Travel to Forest Grove. As long as we successfully explore this location next round, we should be able to retrieve another Athelas.

**Combat:** [[Faramir]] blocks the Dol Guldur Beastmaster. Shadow cards: Black Forest Bats and Mountains of Mirkwood — no additional effect. Faramir takes 1 damage. Aragorn and [[Haldir of Lórien]] counter-attack and kill the Dol Guldur Beastmaster.

**Denethor scry:** Another Athelas on top — obviously keep it.

> We are getting very lucky so far, but luck is sadly somewhat of a requirement in this scenario. If Athelas ends up in the discard pile from either of the Beastmaster's shadow cards, we would be in much worse shape.

*End of round: +2 damage to Wilyador (now 9 damage, 11 hit points remaining). Raise threat.*

### Round 4: Stage 2B (0 of 12 progress)

**31 threat (First Player)**

```
Hand: Empty
— Aragorn: 1 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
— Theodred: 1 resource, 1 damage (3 of 4 hit points)
— Denethor: 1 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)
Active Location: Forest Grove
Staging Area: Athelas (unguarded)
Wilyador: 9 damage (11 of 20 hit points)
```

Draw: Another [[Snowbourn Scout]]. We play the scout and trigger his response to add 1 progress to Forest Grove.

**Quest Phase:** Send Aragorn, Wilyador, [[Haldir of Lórien]], [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]] and the [[Snowbourn Scout]]. Ready Aragorn with Theodred's resource. Trigger Faramir's ability.

**Staging:** The Athelas that Denethor saw is guarded by Driven By Shadow. Edge case: although objectives have no threat, they technically count as cards in the staging area, so Driven By Shadow does not surge. With **11 willpower against no threat**, we explore Forest Grove and add **9 progress to stage 2**.

**Forest Grove response:** Add a third Athelas to the staging area. This copy is guarded by Eyes of the Forest. We have no events in hand, so this treachery is discarded with no effect and our third Athelas is left unguarded.

> This is the luckiest encounter deck draw that I can remember having in a long time, but I am not a bear to turn down free honey-cakes.

**Claiming Athelas:** Aragorn and Theodred each exhaust to claim an Athelas. We save the [[Steward of Gondor]] for its resource ability rather than claiming the third objective right now, to be wary of game-ending treacheries.

**Denethor scry:** King Spider on top — keep it there.

*End of round: +2 damage to Wilyador (now 11 damage, 9 hit points remaining). Raise threat.*

---

### Round 5: Stage 2B (9 of 12 progress)

**32 threat (First Player)**

```
Hand: Empty
— Aragorn: 1 resource, 0 damage (5 of 5 hit points)
— Theodred: 1 resource, 1 damage (3 of 4 hit points)
— Denethor: 3 resource, 0 damage (3 of 3 hit points)
Staging Area: Athelas (unguarded)
Wilyador: 11 damage (9 of 20 hit points)
```

Draw: [[Henamarth Riversong]]. No matter — we have what we need to defeat this scenario.

**Planning:** Play [[Henamarth Riversong]]; might as well let the elf join in the victory celebration. Before the quest phase, exhaust Denethor to claim the third copy of Athelas.

**Quest Phase:** Commit Aragorn, Theodred, Wilyador, [[Haldir of Lórien]], [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]] and [[Henamarth Riversong]]. Ready Aragorn with Theodred's help and trigger Faramir's ability. Total: **14 willpower committed**.

**Staging:** Reveal the King Spider — forces us to exhaust the [[Snowbourn Scout]]. With 14 willpower against 2 threat in the staging area, we make **12 progress on stage 2** and move to **stage 3**.

**Victory!** With three copies of Athelas claimed, we heal all 11 points of damage on Wilyador and **win the game**.

## Score and Recap

> Though our deck was decidedly unhelpful in providing any copies of [[Lore of Imladris]], we were still able to complete the quest and save Wilyador's life. Some fortuitous draws, and help from Denethor, we were able to scrounge enough Athelas that we didn't need any other healing effects.

Because we forgot to heal Theodred with [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]], he still had his 1 damage from those pesky insects. By moving quickly, we completed the quest after only **4 rounds** (rounds tally during the refresh phase) with only **32 threat**. Exploring Rhosgobel was worth 4 victory points, so our final score is:

**32 (threat) + 40 (rounds) + 1 (damage) – 4 (victory) = 69**

## Design Critique and Lessons Learned

While this is an excellent score — particularly limiting ourselves to the core set and three adventure packs — this does feel like somewhat of a hollow victory. The fact of the matter is that we were very fortunate to find 3 copies of Athelas as quickly as we did. Sure, we could have drawn a copy of [[Lore of Imladris]] to save us from having to find and claim the last objective, but things still played out nearly perfectly for us.

> Having faced this scenario several times, it rarely plays out so smoothly. The random factor can be very frustrating as it is quite possible to lose this scenario horribly, even without making any play mistakes.

### Nightmare Version Fix

FFG recently announced Nightmare packs for the first 3 adventure packs of the Shadows of Mirkwood cycle, including this scenario. In the **Nightmare version of [[A Journey to Rhosgobel]]**, Athelas now has a **response that allows you to shuffle it back into the encounter deck** any time it would be placed in the discard pile. This small tweak is an excellent fix to what has always felt like a design oversight with this scenario. Now, having Athelas discarded as a shadow card for an attacking enemy does not have to be a game-ending bit of bad luck.

> For any new players having frustration with this scenario, I definitely recommend that you play with this bit of errata on the Athelas objective.

## Key Strategy Tips

- **Quest aggressively** — every round of delay is 2 more damage on Wilyador
- **Include 3x [[Lore of Imladris]]** — the only non-objective healing available in this card pool
- **Use Denethor's scrying ability relentlessly** — controlling what comes off the encounter deck is crucial in a scenario this luck-dependent
- **Avoid undefended attacks at all cost** — many shadow effects force damage onto Wilyador instead of a hero
- **Include ranged characters** — multiple flying enemies can only be targeted by ranged attackers; [[Haldir of Lórien]] is perfect for this role
- **Collect Athelas as fast as possible** — exhaust characters to claim unguarded objectives rather than leaving them in the staging area
- **Remember the "objectives as staging area cards" ruling** — objectives with no threat still prevent certain treacheries from surging
- **House rule / Nightmare errata:** If Athelas would be discarded as a shadow card, shuffle it back into the encounter deck instead — this makes the scenario far less punishing and closer to fair design
