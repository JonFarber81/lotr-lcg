## Deck Notes and Strategy

### Hero Synergy

The deck is built around [[Dain Ironfoot]]'s passive buff, which grants +1 Attack and +1 Willpower to all ready Dwarf characters. This synergizes with the large number of Dwarf allies to create a wide board state with strong questing and combat.

- **[[Thalin]]** deals 1 damage to each enemy as it is revealed from the encounter deck — particularly effective against weaker enemies in [[Flight from Moria]] and helpful for softening up tougher ones.
- **[[Bifur]]** (Lore) provides resource smoothing, allowing Leadership or Tactics resources to be spent on Lore cards, enabling the deck to splash Lore allies like [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]] and [[Gleowine]] for healing and card draw.

### Resource Acceleration

- **[[Steward of Gondor]]** is the primary resource engine. Attach to [[Dain Ironfoot]] for a reliable flood of Leadership resources.
- **[[Narvi's Belt]]** gives [[Dain Ironfoot]] (Leadership) the ability to pay for cards of any sphere, dramatically expanding the deck's flexibility.

### Card Draw

Card draw was added to improve consistency. [[Gleowine]] and [[Lorien's Wealth]] help cycle through the deck and find key pieces. [[Erebor Record Keeper]] can exhaust to draw a card (by readying via his response), keeping the engine turning. [[Valiant Sacrifice]] generates card draw when allies die in combat.

### Combat Package

- **[[Feint]]** prevents dangerous enemies from attacking, buying time when the combat situation is difficult.
- **[[Quick Strike]]** allows a character to attack an enemy before it attacks — excellent for finishing off enemies engaged with you before they can deal damage.
- **[[Gondorian Spearman]]** deals 1 damage when an enemy engages — pairs well with [[Thalin]]'s damage on reveal and any direct damage effects.
- **[[Dunedain Warning]]** shores up defense, which can be spread across Dwarf defenders for extra staying power.
- **[[Dunedain Watcher]]** cancels shadow effects — critical in [[Flight from Moria]] where shadows can cause devastating direct damage or forced engagement.

### Questing

[[Dain Ironfoot]]'s global buff means even small Dwarf allies contribute significant Willpower. [[Faramir]] can boost the entire fellowship's Willpower for a single round, useful for pushing through a difficult quest stage. [[Protector of Lorien]] can discard cards to add Willpower or Defense to a character.

### Utility

- **[[Henamarth Riversong]]** (×1) provides scrying — exhausting to look at the top card of the encounter deck before the staging step. In [[Flight from Moria]], foreknowledge of the next encounter card is enormously valuable for planning around the Nameless Fear and other brutal encounter effects.
- **[[Miner of the Iron Hills]]** removes Condition attachments, which can appear in the encounter deck.
- **[[Erebor Hammersmith]]** returns an attachment from the discard pile to your hand — useful for recovering key attachments destroyed by encounter effects.

## Quest Overview: Flight from Moria

[[Flight from Moria]] is the third and final quest in the [[Khazad-dûm]] deluxe expansion. It is widely considered one of the most mechanically complex quests in the game, featuring:

- A **Victory Display** mechanic — certain encounter cards that are defeated or bypassed go into a Victory Display, and the accumulated victory points fuel the power of the **Nameless Fear**.
- A **Nameless Fear** — an undefeatable enemy that grows stronger (lower engagement cost and increased attack) as the Victory Display fills up. Players must avoid defeating or bypassing cards that add to the Victory Display, or they risk making the Nameless Fear unmanageable.
- Multiple quest stages with branching paths and forced effects.
- **Blocked by Shadow** — a treachery that forces a player to guess (heads or tails) to avoid a severe penalty, introducing a random element into the quest.
- **Hasty Council** — reveals two quest cards and lets the active player choose one, adding meaningful decisions about quest progression.
- **Narrow Paths** — a quest card whose forced effect lets players search for [[Abandoned Tools]], which are required to complete the quest. Players must decide whether to complete or bypass this stage.
- **Abandoned Tools** — key objective cards needed to escape the mines.

### Key Strategic Considerations

**Managing the Victory Display:**
The Nameless Fear's engagement cost and attack value are tied directly to the number of victory points in the Victory Display. The more victory points accumulate, the sooner the Nameless Fear engages and the harder it hits. Players should prioritize avoiding cards that add victory points wherever possible — even if bypassing or defeating them seems tactically attractive in the short term.

**Bypassing vs. Completing Quest Stages:**
Some quest stages (like Narrow Paths) have powerful forced effects that tempt you to complete them for the benefits (searching for Abandoned Tools). However, completing or bypassing these stages may add victory points to the Victory Display, empowering the Nameless Fear. The author adopted a conservative strategy — bypassing Narrow Paths entirely rather than risking additional victory points, even though fetching Abandoned Tools would accelerate completion of the quest.

> "My reason for bypassing Narrow Paths was to avoid getting any victory points in the victory display. Granted, I could have theoretically placed just one or two progress on the quest so that I could fetch the tools without completing the quest. In any case, I played conservatively to limit the impact of the Nameless Fear. I could see an argument made for taking the risk to get the tools, but this quest has a way of throwing your plans out the window, so I adopted a strategy that I felt would give me the best chance of success without relying on a particular quest card showing up." — Beorn

**The Nameless Fear:**
This enemy cannot be defeated in the normal sense — it must be avoided. Its threat engagement cost drops as the Victory Display grows. Once it engages, it deals heavy attack damage. The best approach is to keep the Victory Display as empty as possible.

**Enemy Accumulation:**
The staging area can fill with enemies quickly. Without the combat efficiency of a Dwarf deck powered by [[Dain Ironfoot]], players can easily become overwhelmed. Engage and defeat enemies steadily rather than allowing them to pile up in staging.

**Shadow Effects and Direct Damage:**
Several encounter cards deal direct damage via shadow effects. [[Dunedain Watcher]] is invaluable for canceling these. [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]] provides healing to keep heroes alive through attrition damage.

**Threat Management:**
If threat climbs too fast, enemies will engage at inopportune moments. [[Bifur]]'s resource-smoothing ability (transferring resources) can be underestimated — consistent access to all sphere cards keeps every turn efficient, and efficiency is what keeps threat from spiraling.

## Scrying and Encounter Deck Prediction

[[Henamarth Riversong]] is highlighted multiple times in the comments as one of the most impactful single cards in this quest. Being able to see the top encounter card before committing characters to the quest allows for:

- Pre-planning combat engagements
- Deciding whether to trigger [[Feint]] or [[Quick Strike]] preemptively
- Knowing when it is safe to over-commit to questing vs. keeping characters ready for combat
- Predicting the Nameless Fear's behavior and engagement timing

## Rules Clarifications (From Comments)

### Hasty Council — Quest Card Choice

When **Hasty Council** instructs players to reveal two quest cards and choose one, the question arose: can you look at the B-side of both quest cards before making your choice?

The intent of the mechanic is to give players meaningful information — the choice would be meaningless without being able to see both sides of the options. The general ruling is that players may examine both cards (including their B-sides) before deciding.

### Action Windows During the Staging Step

The official rulebook's action table marks the "Encounter deck reveals 1 card per player" step with a red box (no actions). However, players can still take actions **after committing characters to the quest** and **before the encounter deck reveals cards** — this window is legal for actions. Once card revelation begins, no actions can be taken until that phase concludes.
