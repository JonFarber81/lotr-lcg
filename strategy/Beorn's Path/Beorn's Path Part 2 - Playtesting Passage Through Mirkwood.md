## Overview

In Part 1 of Beorn's Path, a 40-card deck was built by combining the pre-built Leadership and Lore decks from the Core Set, trimming to the cards that worked well together and covered key strategic needs — resource generation from Leadership, and healing and card draw from the Lore sphere. The first scenario, [[Passage Through Mirkwood]], was also analyzed. Now it is time to playtest the deck against that scenario.

Fantasy Flight rates [[Passage Through Mirkwood]] at difficulty level 1, but their difficulty levels are notoriously unreliable and should be taken with a grain of salt. The goal: successfully navigate the dark forest with a combined Leadership/Lore deck.

---

## Setup and Opening Hand

Heroes placed into play; starting threat set to **28**.

### Opening Hand

Cards drawn: [[Gleowine]], [[Steward of Gondor]], [[Gandalf]], [[Celebrian's Stone]], [[Secret Paths]], [[Guard of the Citadel]]

This is a great hand. With [[Gleowine]] and [[Steward of Gondor]], two of the three main pillars of the deck are represented immediately. Key observations:

- **[[Steward of Gondor]]** enables extra resource generation to help pay for [[Gandalf]] early
- **[[Gandalf]]** is critical for both combat and questing in early rounds
- **[[Celebrian's Stone]]** attached to [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] gives him 4 willpower by himself — vital when [[Theodred]] and [[Denethor]] each have only 1 willpower
- If the hand had not included [[Gleowine]], [[Steward of Gondor]], or a strong combo like [[Gandalf]] + [[Sneak Attack]], a mulligan would have been warranted

**Decision: Keep the hand.**

### Scenario Setup

[[Passage Through Mirkwood]] setup: add one copy of **Forest Spider** and **Old Forest Road** to the staging area.

**Analysis of setup cards:**

- **Forest Spider** (threat threshold 25) — will engage immediately given our starting threat of 28. The Response effect on **Old Forest Road** helps: travelling there first allows us to ready one hero. Combined with [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]'s ability to ready after committing to the quest, we are well prepared for the spider.
- **Old Forest Road** — with a combined threat of 3 plus one unknown encounter card, questing will be difficult in round 1.

**Key early-game deficiency identified:** Until [[Gandalf]] or preferably [[Faramir]] enter play, the deck is not great at questing. With a 28 starting threat, there are a few rounds to build up allies — but the first few rounds are critical. The plan is simply to withstand the initial rush of the encounter deck and survive until reinforcements arrive.

## Round 1: Stage 1B — Threat 28

### Resource Phase

Add one resource to each hero; draw card: **[[Radagast's Cunning]]**

This addresses questing issues somewhat. Both [[Radagast's Cunning]] and [[Secret Paths]] from the opening hand serve as inexpensive ways to deal with staging area threat — think of them as "+X willpower where X is the highest threat enemy or location in the staging area."

### Planning Phase

1. Play [[Steward of Gondor]] — attached to [[Denethor]] (thematically and strategically correct, as he is the only Lore hero). Exhaust to add 2 resources to [[Denethor]]'s pool.
2. Current resources: [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] 0, [[Theodred]] 0, [[Denethor]] 3
3. Spend 2 from [[Denethor]] to play [[Gleowine]] — two of three deck pillars now in play in round 1
4. Save 1 resource on [[Denethor]] for events

### Quest Phase

Characters committed to quest: **everyone except [[Gleowine]]**

- [[Gleowine]] held back — his card-draw ability is too valuable to risk on questing, especially with his vulnerability to [[The Necromancer's Reach]]
- Total willpower committed: **4**

Encounter card revealed: **Dol Guldur Orcs** — a low-threat enemy with a nasty "when revealed" effect.

- Since [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] will likely take an undefended attack soon, have [[Theodred]] take the 2 damage from the "when revealed" effect instead
- Staging area now: 5 threat vs. 4 willpower — don't want to raise threat this early
- Pay the last resource from [[Denethor]] to play [[Radagast's Cunning]] on Dol Guldur Orcs — nullifying their threat for the phase
- With their threat removed, 1 willpower greater than the 3 remaining threat: **place 1 progress on Stage 1**

### Travel Phase

Travel to **Old Forest Road** → ready [[Denethor]] to help with defense.

### Engagement Phase

Both **Forest Spider** and **Dol Guldur Orcs** engage (Forest Spider gets +1 attack from its Forced effect, now attacking for 3).

### Combat Phase

**Assigning blockers:**

- **Forest Spider** (ATK 3) → [[Denethor]] defends
- **Dol Guldur Orcs** → [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] defends (rather than take an undefended risk with [[Gandalf]] in hand as imminent help)

**Shadow cards:**

- Forest Spider shadow: **Eyes of the Forest** — harmless. [[Denethor]] takes no damage.
- Dol Guldur Orcs shadow: **Hummerhorns!** — 1 damage to each character. This illustrates the risk of undefended attacks; if the orcs had gone undefended, Hummerhorns would have dealt 2 damage to everyone and killed [[Theodred]] and [[Gleowine]]. Seeing it first turn is bad luck, but with [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] defending (DEF 2), the attack is handled and no characters die.
- No ready attackers remain for counter-attack.

### End of Round

Exhaust [[Gleowine]] to draw: **[[Son of Arnor]]** — useful, another attacker.

Ready all cards. **Threat raised to 29.**

## Round 2: Stage 1B (1 of 8 progress) — Threat 29

Two enemies engaged; [[Theodred]] has 3 damage. Priority: combat help and healing — urgently.

### Resource Phase

Add resources + exhaust [[Steward of Gondor]]: [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] 1, [[Theodred]] 1, [[Denethor]] 3

Draw: **[[Common Cause]]** — not the healing hoped for, but may be useful.

**Decision:** Use [[Gleowine]] during planning (before quest phase) to try to draw healing — a calculated risk given treacheries that damage exhausted characters, but healing [[Theodred]] is a priority.

Draw from [[Gleowine]]: **[[Protector of Lorien]]** — not healing, but potentially useful.

### Planning Phase

Play [[Celebrian's Stone]] and [[Protector of Lorien]] on [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]. Save 2 resources on [[Denethor]].

### Quest Phase

Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] and [[Theodred]] to the quest. Use [[Theodred]]'s ability to add a resource to [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]], then spend it to ready him.

Encounter card revealed: **Dol Guldur Beastmaster** (threat threshold 35 — will not engage yet).

- [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] now has 5 willpower (via [[Celebrian's Stone]]) vs. Beastmaster's 2 threat: **quest successfully for 3 progress**, exploring **Old Forest Road**.
- [[Protector of Lorien]] discards could push further, but cards are more useful in play than the discard pile at this stage.

### Combat Phase

Two enemies still engaged. Strategy for shadow cards: since [[Hummerhorns]] is already gone, and the biggest attack bonus on a remaining shadow card is +1, it may be time to take one attack undefended.

- [[Denethor]] blocks **Forest Spider** (now attacking for 2)
- **Dol Guldur Orcs** attack goes **undefended**

Shadow cards:
- Forest Spider shadow: **Great Forest Web** → [[Denethor]] survives unscathed
- Dol Guldur Orcs shadow: non-shadow effect → take **2 damage on [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]**

Heroes are looking beaten up — healing is now urgent.

[[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] (not having defended) is ready → kills **Dol Guldur Orcs** with one attack.

### End of Round

Ready all. **Threat raised to 30.**

## Round 3: Stage 1B (1 of 8 progress) — Threat 30

Threat growing, enemies massing — time for the Grey Wizard.

### Resource Phase

Add resources + [[Steward of Gondor]]. Draw: **another [[Gleowine]]**.

Still no healing. Since [[Gleowine]] can be replaced, take the risk and use his ability during planning.

Draw from [[Gleowine]]: **[[Secret Paths]]** — still no healing.

### Planning Phase

**Turning point — make a push to get ahead of the encounter deck:**

1. Pay 5 resources from [[Denethor]] → play [[Gandalf]], who immediately kills the engaged **Forest Spider**
2. Pay 2 Leadership resources → play [[Guard of the Citadel]] for willpower help

### Quest Phase

**Risk assessment:** Dangerous to keep questing with [[Theodred]] at 3 damage, but [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]'s readying ability requires [[Theodred]]'s resource. With [[Gleowine]] drawing extra cards, the hope is that healing arrives before [[The Necromancer's Reach]]. With only 2 threat in staging area, committing everyone to push through is worth the risk.

Commit to quest: [[Theodred]], a readied [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]], [[Guard of the Citadel]]. [[Gandalf]] and [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] hold back to handle the Beastmaster.

Encounter card revealed: **[[The Necromancer's Reach]]!**

> This kills [[Gleowine]] and [[Theodred]], and things are looking pretty grim.

**Post-mortem:** Hindsight is 20/20. Without the extra resource from [[Theodred]], [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] would not have readied — meaning he would have taken damage from [[The Necromancer's Reach]] AND there would not have been enough strength to finish off the Beastmaster before [[Gandalf]] left play. Still, questing with [[Theodred]] at 3 damage was a mistake to learn from. Not drawing any healing also points to a deck deficiency to address.

- 5 committed willpower vs. 2 staging threat: **3 progress** → Stage 1 now at **4 of 8 progress**

### Combat Phase

Optionally engage **Dol Guldur Beastmaster**. [[Denethor]] blocks.

- Shadow cards: **Necromancer's Pass** (harmless) and **Forest Spider** (forces discard of [[Protector of Lorien]])
- [[Denethor]] unscathed; [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] + [[Gandalf]] counter-attack and dispatch the Beastmaster

**Staging area clear.** Lost a hero, but still have a chance.

### End of Round

**Threat raised to 31.** Only 1 Leadership resource per turn going forward — a major challenge.

## Round 4: Stage 1B (4 of 8 progress) — Threat 31

[[Steward of Gondor]] must carry an even heavier load with one less hero.

### Resource Phase

Add resources to [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] and [[Denethor]]. Draw: still no healing!

**[[Son of Arnor]]** is in hand — expensive right now. Save 2 of the 3 [[Denethor]] resources to play a second copy of [[Gleowine]] (since [[Gleowine]] can be replaced). Wait to use his ability until after the quest phase (no [[The Necromancer's Reach]] risk if he's not exhausted during the quest).

### Quest Phase

Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] (does not ready without [[Theodred]]) and [[Guard of the Citadel]].

Encounter card revealed: **Forest Gate** — a low-threat location. A much-needed break.

- 5 willpower vs. 2 threat: **3 more progress** → Stage 1 at **7 of 8 progress**. [[Protector of Lorien]] in the discard pile; otherwise could have pitched [[Son of Arnor]] to complete the stage.

### Travel Phase

Travel to **Forest Gate** → draw **[[Erebor Hammersmith]]** and **[[Faramir]]** (Forest Gate's travel effect).

**Key observation:** The Hammersmith can recover [[Protector of Lorien]] from the discard pile. But **[[Faramir]]** is the critical card — if he can be played, the willpower needed to push through the rest of the scenario is available.

### End of Round (No combat this round)

Use [[Gleowine]] to draw: **[[Forest Snare]]** — useful if [[Ungoliant's Spawn]] must be faced.

Use [[Denethor]]'s scrying ability: **top card of encounter deck is another [[The Necromancer's Reach]]**. With no healing in sight, move it to the bottom of the encounter deck.

**Threat raised to 32.** Faramir can't come soon enough.

## Round 5: Stage 1B (7 of 8 progress) — Threat 32

### Resource Phase

[[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] is saving up for [[Faramir]] — no spending. Draw: **another [[Erebor Hammersmith]]**.

> At this point it is obvious that our deck is taunting us — the [[Daughter of Nimrodel|Daughters of Nimrodel]] are doubtless having a party on the bottom of our deck, most likely accompanied by all of our copies of [[Self Preservation]].

### Planning Phase

Spend all 4 resources from [[Denethor]] → play **both copies of [[Erebor Hammersmith]]**. Advantages:

- Extra blockers who can defend orcs without getting killed
- Trigger the Response on one Hammersmith: return [[Protector of Lorien]] to hand

### Quest Phase

**No [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] ready this round** (can't afford his ability). Commit: one [[Erebor Hammersmith]], [[Guard of the Citadel]], and one more character.

Encounter card revealed: **Caught in a Web!**

- [[Denethor]] is the logical choice to get tangled — [[Steward of Gondor]] gives him 3 resources a turn regardless. The attachment can potentially be managed, but a [[Miner of the Iron Hills]] would be ideal.

**Successfully quested for 6 progress** → explores **Forest Gate** and **completes Stage 1 of the quest!**

Stage 2 has only **2 quest points** — things are looking up.

### End of Round

No enemies to engage. No reason to exhaust [[Denethor]] (would tangle him further with Caught in a Web).

Use [[Gleowine]] to draw: **[[Miner of the Iron Hills]]!**

> Yes, yes, yes. After 5 rounds of taunts and jeers, our deck has finally delivered.

Now have: a solution to **Caught in a Web**, and [[Faramir]] ready to be paid for next round.

**Threat raised to 33.**

## Round 6: Stage 2B — Threat 33

### Resource Phase

Draw: **[[Common Cause]]** — not exciting, but may be useful.

### Planning Phase

[[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] is still saving for [[Faramir]]. [[Denethor]] pays for **[[Miner of the Iron Hills]]** → the Dwarf promptly untangles **Caught in a Web**, freeing [[Denethor]].

### Quest Phase

Commit [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]], [[Guard of the Citadel]], and one [[Erebor Hammersmith]].

Encounter card revealed: **East Bight Patrol** — much preferred as an enemy than as a shadow card. With only 1 DEF and 2 hit points, easily dispatched.

- 6 willpower vs. 3 threat: **complete Stage 2!**

### Reaching Stage 3 — Random Selection

Stage 3 is one of two options, selected at random:

- **Don't Leave the Path** — no quest points, requires fetching a Spider and defeating [[Ungoliant's Spawn]] before claiming victory
- **Beorn's Path** — 10 quest points, only have to fight [[Ungoliant's Spawn]] if she is revealed before the stage is completed

After shuffling and random selection: **Beorn's Path!**

> Never could there be a more poetic ending. Our first attempt at playtesting will finish on a quest stage named after this very series of articles and your friendly neighborhood bear. We have to win this scenario now, Eru Ilúvatar has willed it.

### Combat Phase

**East Bight Patrol** engages. Ready [[Erebor Hammersmith]] blocks.

- Shadow card: another **Caught in a Web** — ineffectual. Hammersmith takes 2 damage but survives.

Play **[[Common Cause]]** → exhaust [[Denethor]] to ready [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]. [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]] dispatches the orcs.

Use [[Gleowine]] to draw: **[[Secret Paths]]** — may be useful next round.

**Threat raised to 34.**

## Round 7: Stage 3B (Beorn's Path) — Threat 34

Because we kept our cool and didn't wander aimlessly through Mirkwood, we were rewarded with the easier of the two final stages. Only [[Ungoliant's Spawn]] being revealed can prevent victory — and [[Denethor]]'s scrying ability lets us see the top card and put it on the bottom if needed.

### Resource Phase

Draw: **[[Sneak Attack]]** — now meaningless, but at least it arrived.

Use **[[Denethor]]'s scrying ability**: top card is another **Dol Guldur Beastmaster** — leave it there. Knowing 2 threat will be in the staging area, we just need to quest for at least **12** willpower to win.

### Planning Phase

Pay the hard-saved **4 resources** → play **[[Faramir]]**.

### Quest Phase

Trigger **[[Faramir]]'s ability** → all other characters get +1 willpower until end of phase.

Commit **everyone except [[Denethor]] and [[Faramir]]** (both exhausted).

Total willpower: **14** vs. 2 threat from Dol Guldur Beastmaster.

> **We win the game.**

## Lessons Learned

### 1. Don't quest with wounded heroes

Questing with [[Theodred]] at 3 damage was a mistake. When a hero is one treachery away from death, they should be held back — even at the cost of willpower. The trade-off (losing [[Theodred]]'s resource generation) is better than losing the hero.

### 2. The deck needs more healing

2 copies each of [[Daughter of Nimrodel]] and [[Self Preservation]] proved insufficient. The entire playthrough passed without drawing a single healing card. This highlights a serious deck deficiency.

> Between the combat, treacheries like [[The Necromancer's Reach]], and shadow card nastiness, healing is a very dire need for this deck.

This is especially true because Leadership is not as strong at combat as Tactics, so heroes tend to take more damage over the course of a game.

### 3. Steward of Gondor is essential

Resource acceleration via [[Steward of Gondor]] on [[Denethor]] was the backbone of the deck throughout. Without it, plays like [[Gandalf]], [[Faramir]], and both [[Erebor Hammersmith]]s in the same turn would have been impossible.

### 4. Denethor's scrying is underrated

[[Denethor]]'s ability to look at the top card of the encounter deck — and move dangerous cards like [[The Necromancer's Reach]] to the bottom — proved invaluable for avoiding game-ending treacheries in the late game.

### 5. Location management matters

**Old Forest Road**'s Response (ready a hero after travelling there) combined with [[Aragorn (Leadership)|Aragorn]]'s readying ability provided vital flexibility for both questing and combat in the early rounds.

### 6. Know your shadow cards

After seeing [[Hummerhorns]] in Round 1, it was safe in Round 2 to let attacks go undefended knowing the worst remaining shadow effects were +1 attack bonuses. Tracking what has already been revealed informs risk calculations.

### 7. [[Faramir]] is the key late-game card

Once [[Faramir]] hit the table in Round 7, the game was essentially over. Planning the resource curve around playing him at the right moment was critical.
