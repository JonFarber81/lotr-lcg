## Introduction

In this installment of Beorn's Path, we tackle [[The Dead Marshes]] — a scenario from the [[Shadows of Mirkwood]] cycle that continues the hunt for Gollum. This is a unique and challenging scenario driven by the escape mechanic: Gollum accumulates resources each round, and players must pass escape tests to keep him from fleeing. Aggressive questing and careful encounter deck management are essential to success.

The deck used here is the Leadership/Lore build that has been refined throughout the series, piloted by **Aragorn**, **Theodred**, and **Denethor**.

## Deck Overview

The Leadership/Lore deck is built around a core strategic axis: establish board presence quickly with inexpensive allies, ramp up resource generation with [[Steward of Gondor]], and then quest aggressively using [[Faramir]]'s willpower-boosting ability. The deck contains several card categories, each serving a distinct function.

---

## Card Categories and Inclusions

### Resource Generation

**[[Steward of Gondor]] (x2)** — The gold standard of resource generation. Attach to [[Denethor (Lore)]] to maximize value, allowing him to pay for Leadership cards. Combined with [[Song of Kings]], this creates a highly flexible resource engine.

**[[Song of Kings]] (x1)** — Allows [[Denethor (Lore)]] to pay for Leadership cards. Since [[Rivendell Minstrel]] can fetch it from anywhere in the deck, a single copy is sufficient.

**[[Song of Wisdom]] (x1)** — Similar resource-smoothing function; provides Lore access where needed.

**[[Rivendell Minstrel]] (x3)** — Fetches songs while also contributing 2 willpower to questing. After the song is retrieved, the Minstrel becomes a reliable questing ally, effectively functioning as a delayed "grunt." Their ability to smooth resources between the two spheres is invaluable in a two-sphere deck.

> **Tip:** For resource smoothing in multi-sphere decks, Songs + Rivendell Minstrel creates a package that handles both resource access and card advantage simultaneously.

---

### Card Draw

**[[Gleowine]] (x2)** — Described as "an absolute bargain at 2 resources for repeatable card draw." Exhausting Gleowine for a card each round quickly offsets his cost. He is best attached (via commitment) to questing when the card draw is less critical so as not to waste the action.

**[[Valiant Sacrifice]] (x2)** — Situational but necessary at this stage of the campaign. Triggers when an ally leaves play, converting "grunt" deaths into card advantage.

**[[Campfire Tales]] (x2)** — Less efficient than dedicated draw engines, but helps shrink the deck and access critical cards earlier. Particularly useful in multi-player, but still provides value in solo.

---

### Questing Power

**[[Faramir]] (x2)** — The lynchpin of the deck's questing strategy. Each round, he boosts the willpower of every committed character by +1, turning a board full of inexpensive "grunt" allies into a powerful questing force. Drawing Faramir mid-game is often the turning point of a scenario.

**[[Celebrian's Stone]] (x1)** — Attached to [[Aragorn (Leadership)]], this raises his willpower from 2 to 4 and provides access to the Spirit sphere. A single copy suffices because it is unique and [[Rivendell Minstrel]] cannot fetch it.

**[[Protector of Lórien]] (x2)** — Attached to [[Aragorn (Leadership)]], allows discarding cards to boost willpower or defense. Provides burst questing power in critical rounds and works synergistically with hand-management strategies. Can also boost defense when needed.

**[[Dúnedain Quest]] (x2)** — Adds willpower directly to a hero. Provides a permanent, stackable willpower boost without needing to commit the hero, which is useful when [[Aragorn (Leadership)]] is needed for escape tests.

---

### Combat

**[[Dúnedain Mark]] (x2)** — Boosts [[Aragorn (Leadership)]]'s attack. Cheap at 1 resource, not unique, and not restricted. Loading two copies onto Aragorn significantly improves his ability to destroy enemies without requiring additional attackers.

**[[A Burning Brand]] (x2)** — Attached to [[Denethor (Lore)]], this allows him to cancel shadow effects when defending. Removes the randomness from combat and lets Denethor defend reliably throughout the game. A core defensive card.

**[[Forest Snare]] (x2)** — Engages overwhelming enemies and prevents them from attacking. Particularly useful for large enemies that would otherwise dominate combat, or in situations where the player cannot afford to kill an enemy immediately.

---

### Allies: Grunts

Inexpensive allies that can quest, chump block, or absorb damage. Their individual combat stats are low, but their collective value with [[Faramir]] in play is enormous.

**[[Guard of the Citadel]] (x3)** — 2-cost Leadership ally with 1 willpower and 1 attack. The backbone of the questing force. Becomes 2 willpower with Faramir active.

**[[Erebor Hammersmith]] (x2)** — 2-cost Lore ally with 1 willpower. Can return a restricted attachment from the discard pile, providing occasional recursion. Another reliable questing body.

> **Tip:** Grunts are the deck's primary questing engine. Fill the board with them early, then activate Faramir to multiply their willpower. This "mass questing" strategy clears stages quickly and prevents encounter cards from building up.

---

### Allies: Heavies

High-impact allies reserved for difficult situations or key combo plays.

**[[Haldir of Lórien]] (x1)** — A powerful ranged attacker and quester. His ranged keyword is not useful in solo, but his 2 attack and 2 willpower make him a flexible mid-game threat. Included as a single copy because he is unique.

**[[Gildor Inglorion]] (x1)** — Unique Lore ally with the ability to look at and rearrange the top three cards of your deck. Synergizes directly with [[Denethor (Lore)]]'s scrying ability. Lets you plan multiple rounds ahead with precise information.

**[[Gandalf]] (x3)** — The ultimate flexible ally. Provides 4 willpower, 4 attack, 4 defense, and 5 hit points for a single round. On entry, choose one: deal 4 damage to an enemy, draw 3 cards, or reduce threat by 5. [[Sneak Attack]] allows playing Gandalf for only 1 Leadership resource. Three copies maximize the chance of drawing him when needed.

---

### Allies: Location Control

**[[Snowbourn Scout]] (x3)** — 1-cost Leadership ally who places 1 progress on a location when he enters play. Used to preemptively chip away at troublesome locations, preventing location lock. His value is primarily in his entry ability; after that, he becomes a questing body or chump blocker.

---

### Events: Location Management

**[[Secret Paths]] (x2)** — Reduces a location's threat to 0 for the questing phase. Allows the player to ignore high-threat locations temporarily, reducing staging area pressure.

**[[Strider's Path]] (x2)** — After traveling to a location, place 1 progress on the current quest. Also allows travel to any location with the Scout trait, bypassing normal travel restrictions. The progress placement can be decisive in close questing situations.

---

### Healing

**[[Daughter of the Nimrodel]] (x3)** — 2-cost Lore ally with 0 willpower and 0 attack. Her sole function is healing: she can heal 2 damage from a hero each round. Since the three heroes are all fragile (particularly [[Denethor (Lore)]] who regularly defends), keeping them alive through sustained combat is critical. Three copies maximize the chance of having at least one in play at all times.

> **Warning:** Daughter of the Nimrodel has only 1 hit point, making her vulnerable to any area-of-effect damage from encounter cards or shadow effects. Protect her or be prepared to replace her.

---

### Utility

**[[Henamarth Riversong]] (x1)** — Unique Lore ally who exhausts to look at the top card of the encounter deck. Works in concert with [[Denethor (Lore)]]'s scrying ability. Scout the top card with Henamarth, then use Denethor's response to move it to the bottom if it is a threat treachery or a dangerous escape card. Combined, these two effects can dramatically reduce the variance of the encounter deck.

**[[Sneak Attack]] (x2)** — Plays an ally from hand into play for only 1 Leadership resource, then returns them at the end of the phase. Primarily used with [[Gandalf]] to get his powerful entry effect without paying 5 resources. Can also be used with [[Snowbourn Scout]] for emergency progress placement, or with [[Daughter of the Nimrodel]] for emergency healing.

**[[Grim Resolve]] (x1)** — A unique Leadership event that readies all characters. In resource-rich situations (particularly when [[Steward of Gondor]] has been generating surplus), this provides a massive action advantage: every hero and ally can quest, then ready and fight. A single copy is sufficient as a situational finisher.

## The Dead Marshes: Scenario Overview and Mechanics

[[The Dead Marshes]] is a scenario built around the escape mechanic, which replaces willpower with attack strength for a special kind of progress test. The core challenge:

- **Gollum** begins in the staging area with 5 hit points and accumulates resources each round.
- Each round, players must pass an **escape test**: reveal cards from the encounter deck and compare their combined escape value against the players' total attack strength. If the total escape value is lower than the players' attack, the test passes and Gollum does not flee.
- If Gollum accumulates enough resources (via failed or marginal escape tests), he escapes and the players lose.

**Key strategic implications:**

1. **Aggressive questing is critical.** The longer the scenario lasts, the more resources Gollum accumulates and the harder escape tests become. Clear quest stages quickly.

2. **Escape test management.** [[Denethor (Lore)]] and [[Henamarth Riversong]] synergize powerfully here: scout the top card with Henamarth before the escape test, then use Denethor's response to move dangerous high-escape-value cards to the bottom of the encounter deck.

3. **Board presence prevents snowballing.** Establishing a full board of allies early means more characters to commit to escape tests, more attackers for enemies, and more willpower for questing. The sooner board superiority is established, the easier the scenario becomes.

> **Key principle:** "The longer that you wait to establish board superiority, the harder it can become." Punch the scenario in the mouth before it knows what happened.

## Key Strategic Takeaways

### 1. Establish Board Superiority Immediately

The single most important strategic principle for [[The Dead Marshes]] is speed. Gollum accumulates resources every round — the longer the game goes, the stronger the escape tests become. Deploy inexpensive allies in Round 1 and 2 to build a questing and escape-test force before the encounter deck can apply pressure.

> "Punch it in the mouth before it even knows what happened."

### 2. Scrying Synergy Between Denethor and Henamarth

The combination of [[Denethor (Lore)]] and [[Henamarth Riversong]] is uniquely powerful in [[The Dead Marshes]]:

1. Use Henamarth to scout the top card of the encounter deck.
2. If the top card has a high escape value or dangerous effect, use Denethor's response to move it to the bottom.
3. This lets you effectively choose which escape cards you face, drastically reducing escape test variance.

Use Henamarth before committing to the escape test, not after — the order of operations matters.

### 3. Aragorn is the Swiss Army Knife

[[Aragorn (Leadership)]] fulfills multiple roles in this scenario:

- Primary quester (high willpower, boosted further by [[Celebrian's Stone]] and [[Dúnedain Quest]])
- Escape test contributor (high attack, boosted by [[Dúnedain Mark]])
- Secondary attacker for enemies

He needs to be available for escape tests when they occur, so plan his readying carefully. [[Theodred]]'s resource-generation response readies Aragorn each planning phase, giving him additional action economy.

### 4. Faramir as the Force Multiplier

[[Faramir]] transforms a board of inexpensive "grunt" allies into a questing army. A board with [[Guard of the Citadel]], [[Erebor Hammersmith]], [[Gleowine]], and [[Henamarth Riversong]] goes from ~5 willpower to ~9 willpower with Faramir active. Drawing him in Round 3 of this playthrough was the game-defining moment.

### 5. The Leadership/Lore Combination

The Leadership/Lore sphere pairing provides:

- Superior willpower (Lore allies, questing attachments, card draw to assemble the questing package)
- Resource generation (Steward of Gondor, Theodred)
- Encounter deck manipulation (Denethor, Henamarth, Gildor)
- Healing (Daughter of the Nimrodel)

This combination handles [[The Dead Marshes]] better than a Spirit-heavy build because attack strength (needed for escape tests) is more naturally available in Leadership, while Lore provides the scrying tools to manage escape test variance.

### 6. Resource Management Discipline

With only two resource-generating heroes initially, resource management is critical:

- Attach [[Steward of Gondor]] to [[Denethor (Lore)]] early so he can pay for Leadership cards.
- Use [[Sneak Attack]] to get [[Gandalf]]'s entry effects for only 1 resource in clutch situations.
- Play grunts before heavies — establish the board with cheap allies before committing resources to expensive ones.
