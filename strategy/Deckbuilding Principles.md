# Deckbuilding Principles

> Source: [Vision of the Palantir](https://visionofthepalantir.com/2024/08/12/deckbuilding-principles/) by durinsfather (Aug 2024)

---

## 1. Conceptual Design

Every deck starts with an idea. Before touching cards, answer as many of these as you can:

- Solo or multiplayer?
- What is the main sphere?
- Is there a trait, contract, or mechanic you want to build around?
- What will the weaknesses of your deck be? (You don't have to solve this yet.)
- What quest do you want to beat?
- Do you care about theme?

Keep this concept in mind throughout the build — it guides every card decision.

---

## 2. Hero Selection

You can select **up to 3 heroes**. Contracts change this:
- [[The Grey Wanderer]] → 1 hero
- [[At the End of All Things]] → 2 heroes
- [[Bond of Friendship]] → up to 4 heroes

**Key rules of thumb:**

- **Always start with 3 heroes** unless you have a specific Secrecy plan. Even for Secrecy, start with [[Folco Boffin]] (use his resource turn 1, then discard him for −1 starting threat).
- **Cover the three solo roles**: Questing, Defending, Attacking. A classic example: [[Éowyn (Spirit)|Éowyn]] (quest) + [[Beregond]] (defend) + [[Legolas]] (attack).
- **Diversify stats**: Heroes with a 2/2/2 statline can cover multiple roles if your specialist falls. Pure specialists are efficient but fragile.
- **Starting threat should be 26–32**. Higher than that and you'll engage enemies too early and get close to elimination fast. Watch out for quest-specific thresholds (e.g., [[Journey Along the Anduin]] punishes starting at 30+).

**Questions to ask:**
- How will I handle the first quest phase with only heroes?
- Can I survive an enemy attack on turn 1?
- Is my starting threat going to cause problems with this quest?

## 3. Selecting Cards

### Contracts

Lock in your contract during conceptual design — it constrains which card types you can include (e.g., [[Burglar's Turn]] = no attachments; [[Fellowship]] = no non-unique allies). Changing contracts mid-build forces painful removals.

*Tip:* If you end up with almost no attachments anyway, [[Burglar's Turn]] is easy to bolt on — fill the treasure deck with Guarded attachments as a free bonus.

---

### Allies

Fill the gaps your heroes can't cover. Priority order:

1. **Cheap questers** — if your heroes are combat-focused. Examples: Escort from Edoras, Silvan Refugee, Eagle Emissary.
2. **Early defenders** — undefended attacks kill heroes fast. Prioritise defenders over attackers. Options: Snowbourn Scout (chump), [[Defender of Rammas]], [[Winged Guardian]], Jubayr, [[Defender of Cair Andros]].
3. **Attackers** — you can often save up for heavy hitters and rely on hero base stats early.
4. **Utility allies** — card draw ([[Gleowine]]), healing ([[Warden of Healing]]), resource gen (Halfast Gamgee), threat reduction (Spirit Elfhelm), deck manipulation ([[Galadriel]], [[Imladris Stargazer]], [[Master of the Forge]]).

*Note:* Quests that start you engaged or require Battle-questing demand early attack power — plan accordingly.

---

### Events

Use events as **glue** — they fix consistency and provide one-off answers (shadow cancellation, emergency questing, etc.). Some archetypes (Silvan, Noldor, Doomed) are event-heavy by design; 15–20 events is fine in those cases.

- You **don't need 3 copies** of every event. Events can be recurred with Record attachments, [[Dwarven Tomb]], or [[Will of the West]].
- [[Council of the Wise]] contract forces 1 copy of each card — lean into recursion.
- Flip through your collection and pick events that fit *your* deck's specific gaps.

---

### Attachments

Attachments enhance heroes and allies. Key considerations:

- **Match attachments to roles**: questers → more willpower or [[Windfola]]; attackers → weapons; defenders → defence/hitpoints boosts.
- **Generic utility heroes** benefit most from readying attachments (get their ability + boosted stats).
- **Trap decks** use attachments on the table instead of on characters — this costs deck space, so balance carefully.
- Attachments can be recurred cheaply: [[Reforged]], [[Second Breakfast]], Erebor Hammersmith — so you don't always need 3 copies.
- [[Master of the Forge]] and Haleth can tutor attachments from your deck.
- In **multiplayer**: communicate your attachment plans beforehand — unique attachments (Legacy of Durin, King under the Mountain) clash badly between two Dwarf decks.

---

### Side-Quests

Unless you're building around [[Thurindir]], include **at most 2 side-quests**.

Two flavors:
- **One-time buff**: lower threat, search your deck for a card.
- **Persistent rule**: e.g., +1 willpower to all heroes, or play the first ally each turn without a sphere match.

Useful picks by situation:
- **[[The Storm Comes]]** — tri/quad-sphere decks that need sphere flexibility.
- **[[Prepare for Battle]]** — if you lack card draw in solo or 2-player.

Only include 4+ side-quests if you're building a Thurindir side-quest deck and certain you can explore them all.

## 4. Deck Balance

Two critical balance principles:

**Cost curve** — most cards should cost 0–2 resources. If you have many 3+ cost cards:
- Find ways to reduce cost
- Spread cost across multiple heroes (use neutral [[Song]] attachments to grant sphere icons)
- Reconsider the expensive cards
- Exception: mono-sphere decks generate 3+ resources of one sphere per turn, so can afford a higher cost curve.

**Card draw** — a normal game is ~10 turns. With a 6-card opening hand and no draw, you'll see only 16 cards. You need reliable card draw to access your key cards. Every archetype has draw options — include them.

## 5. Cutting to 50 Cards

The 50-card minimum is optimal. A 60-card deck is statistically worse at finding your key combo pieces.

**Before cutting — alternatives to consider:**
- **Too many good cards?** Split into two decks and play two-handed.
- **Love your 80-card pile?** Use [[Perilous Voyage]] (min 100 cards) — but remove all threat reduction and prepare for a dangerous late game.

**How to cut:**

1. **Remove duplicate solutions** — if you have 2 different ways to cancel attacks, do you really need both?
2. **Drop third copies of unique cards** — you can only play 1 copy at a time. 2 copies is usually enough, especially with card draw. Drop to 1 if you have strong deck-digging (Mirror of Galadriel, Gather Information).
3. **Cutting 55 → 50**: put the last 5 cuts in a sideboard instead of discarding them. Swap them in per-quest as needed.

*Optional tip:* Many experienced players run 51 cards — a single "fun of it" card that rarely sees play but is a pleasant surprise in multiplayer.

*Note:* Noldor decks with heavy card draw can comfortably run 55–57 cards and see everything before the game ends.

## 6. Playtesting

Test against a variety of quests to find weaknesses. Suggested baseline quests for general-purpose testing:

| Quest | What it tests |
|---|---|
| [[Journey Along the Anduin]] | Early enemy pressure, threat management |
| [[Into the Pit]] | Location and encounter pressure |
| [[The Mumakil]] | Enemy surge, combat efficiency |
| [[The Drowned Ruins]] | Location control |
| [[Foundations of Stone]] | Encounter deck variance |
| [[Journey Up the Anduin]] | Straightforward benchmark |
| [[The Treachery of Rhudaur]] | Condition/treachery handling |
| [[Escape from Umbar]] | Speed and enemy engagement |
| [[Journey in the Dark]] | Combat-heavy pressure |
| [[The Oath]] + [[The Caves of Nibin-Dum]] | Multi-stage challenge |

Test mono-theme decks (e.g., mono-Spirit willpower burst) against quests suited to their strengths *and* weaknesses. Iterate — the game is hard, and encounter decks have their own combos.

## 7. Publishing (Optional)

If you're happy with the deck, share it on [RingsDB](https://ringsdb.com):

- Choose a funny or descriptive name.
- Write a description covering your design thought process and why key cards are included.
- Share playtesting results — which quests it beat, where it struggled.
- Be open to feedback; publish a v2 if you get useful tips.

---

## Quick Reference Checklist

- [ ] Concept defined (solo/multiplayer, sphere, mechanic, target quest)
- [ ] 3 heroes selected; starting threat 26–32
- [ ] Questing, defending, and attacking roles covered (heroes + early allies)
- [ ] Cheap questers and early defenders in ally lineup
- [ ] Card draw included
- [ ] Cost curve mostly 0–2 resources
- [ ] Deck cut to 50 cards (or deliberate exception made)
- [ ] Sideboard of 5 flex cards prepared
- [ ] Tested against at least 2–3 varied quests
