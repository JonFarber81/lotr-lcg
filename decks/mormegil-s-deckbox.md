**<FONT COLOR="#526C32"><font size="+3">1. Premise**</font></font>

---

---

<p align="justify">After I started playing this game, it soon became apparent that there were two aspects of the game I enjoyed more than all other aspects, even more than playing: The community interactions around the game and deckbuilding. In accordance to these preferences, my list of decks had grown pretty large and extremely unorganized. I therefore wanted to take the end of official player card releases as an opportunity to update, categorize, finalize and publish my decks to have a “deckbox” of sorts on ringsdb, that myself (and other players if so inclined) can turn to when looking for a certain theme, archetype, role, synergy, combo or playing style to bring to the table. </p>

<p align="justify">This is of course a very long and time-intensive undertaking, however I am now at a stage where – while not finished – I decided to start publishing what I came up with. So expect this deck description (as well as the list of links for decks posted below) to grow and  be updated continuously over the foreseeable future. One aspect of this is of course that you can influence the project since it is by no means done – if you have suggestions on specific decks, considerations for the project in a broader sense or want to request an archetype being posted sooner rather than later, feel free to reach out in the comments down below (or on Discord).</p>

<p align="justify">It is also important to mention that other people already have contributed in a significant way to this deckbox. Two I would like to call out specifically are Jban and Rouxxor – two good friends I made playing the game that also happen to be excellent deckbuilders. Pretty much every deck listed down below has been seen by and discussed with them. During that process, a lot of decks improved due to their valuable input and deckbuilding expertise. This project would have been a lot more cumbersome and of a much lower quality without their company!</p>

**<FONT COLOR="#526C32"><font size="+3">2. Deck Design</font></font>**

---

---

### **In each archetype, I built four kinds of decks:**

---

- <p align="justify">One Solo deck – the most powerful deck of that archetype I could come up with for solo play.</p>

<p></p>

- <p align="justify">One Multiplayer deck – the most powerful deck of that archetype I could come up with for a multiplayer setting.</p>

<p></p>
 
- <p align="justify">One Thematic deck – a thematic deck that presents the theme of the archetype in a prominent way (while remaining as functional as possible). If an archetype has no real theme (e.g. Caldara decks), there will be no thematic deck.</p>

<p></p>

- <p align="justify">Any number of Alternative decks – here I explore ideas different to the most powerful versions. They are therefore by default not as strong (although sometimes only very marginally weaker), however often more fun, diverse and creative.</p>

<p></p>

- <p align="justify">If there is an idea that needs more than one deck to accomplish it, I might built a Fellowship exploring that idea. Since there are exponentially more Fellowships to be built than decks (and thus creating a catalogue for them is almost impossible), I will not build them for any idea I have (as I do with decks), but much more seldom if a particular idea really intrigues me.</p>

---

### **Sideboards**

---

I stuck to a few rules concerning my sideboards.

<p align="justify">There are no second or third copies of cards already in the deck in the sideboard – so when swapping cards in or out, cards that are once or twice in the deck automatically can and should also be considered as options in addition to the sideboard.</p>

<p align="justify">Every solo deck has three cards in the sideboard, every other deck five. I try to explain how and when the cards of the sideboard are meant to be used in the respective deck descriptions under "Considerations".</p>

**<p align="justify">If a thematic or alternative deck is geared towards solo and multiplayer, I included your typical solo choices (like [[Henamarth Riversong]]) in the deck with the sideboard containing options to enable a better experience for multiplayer.</p>**

**<FONT COLOR="#526C32"><font size="+3">3. Preferred Player Count and Rating</font></font>**

---

---

<p align="justify">The Preferred Player Count and Rating for a deck can be found in the beginning of each deck description. </p>

<p align="justify">The former is meant to indicate – who could have guesses – the preferred player count the deck was built for :). If a certain number is set in brackets, the deck will be functional in that player count but is not directly built with it in mind. Here is an example: </p>

**Preferred Player Count: 2-3, (4)** 

- The deck is most efficient and built for a 2-/3-player-game
- The deck is not built with a 4-player-game in mind but still useful in one 
- The deck is not very useful for a solo-game

<p align="justify">The latter should hopefully give you an idea concerning the power of the deck. </p>

**The rating is scaled as follows:**

<p align="justify"><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span>=A solo deck that can win almost every quests with ease/a multiplayer deck way above curve</p>

<p></p>

<p align="justify"><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span>=A very strong deck that will lead you to victory more often than not</p>

<p></p>

<p align="justify"><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span>=A deck that can hold its own very well, however it might struggle against very difficult quests</p>

<p></p>

<p align="justify"><span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span>=A deck that can contribute, however be prepared to record quite a few losses as well</p>

<p></p>

<p align="justify"><span class="icon-unique"></span>=A deck that is ultimately more novel (in terms of fun, theme or interaction) than efficient</p>

**<p align="justify">Note that the scaling method here are quests in Nightmare mode – so a deck with a rating of <span class="icon-unique"></span><span class="icon-unique"></span> should hopefully still beat most normal quests.</p>**

**<p align="justify">Fellowships will be graded by the same metrics, although their preferred player count will logically never drop below 2.</p>**

<p align="justify">This table should hopefully provide you with a more in-depth analysis of the decks, their role as well as their strengths and weaknesses. Here is an overview what each column indicates:</p>

<p></p>

- **Deck Type:** The type of deck (i.e. solo, multiplayer, thematic, alternative)

<p></p>

- **Deck Name/Link:** The name of the deck in which its ringsdb-link is embedded

<p></p>

- **PPC:** The Preferred Player Count for the deck (as explained above)

<p></p>

- **<span class="icon-unique"></span>:** The Power Rating for the deck from 1-5 (as explained above)

<p></p>

- **<span class="icon-willpower"></span> :** The willpower capabilities of the deck ranked from 0-5

<p></p>

- **<span class="icon-attack"></span> :** The attacking capabilities of the deck ranked from 0-5

<p></p>

- **<span class="icon-defense"></span> :** The defensive capabilities of the deck ranked from 0-5

<p></p>

- <p align="justify"> <span class="icon-health"></span>: The supporting capabilities of the deck ranked from 0-5. This is a little  vague but includes numerous things such as healing, location control, scrying, encounter deck manipulation, cancelling capabilities as well as the ability to support other players with cards or resources</p>

<p align="justify">The table should also give you an indication on which decks to pair and which decks to play in which situations. For that, I use the <span class="icon-willpower"></span>, <span class="icon-attack"></span>, <span class="icon-defense"></span> and <span class="icon-health"></span> capabilities ranked from 0-5.</p>

**<p align="justify">These are the capability numbers I target for a very difficult quests added up for all decks in play:</p>**


- **In 1P-games, I target values of: <span class="icon-willpower"></span> ≥ 2, <span class="icon-attack"></span> ≥ 1, <span class="icon-defense"></span> ≥ 2, <span class="icon-health"></span> ≥ 1**

<p></p>

- **In 2P-games, I target values of: <span class="icon-willpower"></span> ≥ 4, <span class="icon-attack"></span> ≥ 2, <span class="icon-defense"></span> ≥ 3, <span class="icon-health"></span> ≥ 2**

<p></p>

- **In 3P-games, I target values of: <span class="icon-willpower"></span> ≥ 6, <span class="icon-attack"></span> ≥ 4, <span class="icon-defense"></span> ≥ 5, <span class="icon-health"></span> ≥ 4**

<p></p>

- **In 4P-games, I target values of: <span class="icon-willpower"></span> ≥ 8, <span class="icon-attack"></span> ≥ 6, <span class="icon-defense"></span> ≥ 6, <span class="icon-health"></span> ≥ 7**

<p align="justify">These numbers are of course not applicable to all quests and all fellowships, they are more meant as an approximation/a rule of thumb to help whenever problems occur. In higher player counts, I mostly (but not always!) prefer running specialized decks to having decks that all cover a bit of everything.</p>

**<FONT COLOR="#526C32"><font size="+3">7. An Offline Deckbox – 16 Decks Were Given to the Race of Men</font></font>**

---

---

<p align="justify">Some time ago, Jban and myself challenged ourselves to build a set of 16 decks that can all be built with one complete collection offline.</p>

<p align="justify">The aim was to make the decks accessible, fun and diverse for newer players and veterans of the game while still being strong enough to compete against (almost) all quests in the game (be it in easy, normal or nightmare mode). We wanted this set of decks to keep a playing group entertained for a long time, to be suitable to introduce new players to the game and to be a viable option to take to conventions of any level.</p>
